# Learnings: pre-call-email-writer

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-09 · job 150001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- This skill's philosophy ("the email IS the consumable," hundreds to 1,000+ words) is calibrated to the 72-hour post-booking window, but it gets reached for on the 14-day pre-booking community nurture too, where the P-group email-machine doctrine (plain text, short, skimmable in 20 seconds) is the better fit. A one-line note distinguishing "post-booking pre-call (go long, install beliefs)" from "pre-booking community nurture (go short, get the click)" would stop the wrong length from being applied.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180003 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The skill's whole philosophy ("the email IS the consumable," hundreds to 1,000+ words, 14 emails over a 72-hour window) is calibrated to the post-booking pre-call window, but this skill is the one whose name gets matched for the orchestrator's 14-DAY pre-booking community nurture, which is a different animal: short, P-group, skimmable in 20 seconds, one email per DAY. The SKILL.md should carry a one-line disambiguation at the top distinguishing "post-booking pre-call (go long, install beliefs, 72 hours)" from "pre-booking community nurture (go short, get the click, 14 days, governed by emails-and-booking.md)," so the wrong cadence and length aren't applied.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180004 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The orchestrator now splits the call funnel's email work into separate deliverables (community nurture, post-booking show-up, no-show/post-call recovery, SMS), so a single run often commissions only the show-up subset (roughly 5 to 7 emails), not the full flow. The skill's hard rule ("fixed at 14 emails over 72 hours, do not improvise the slot count") and its Step 5 checklist ("all 14 slots filled, timed at hour 0, +1, +3, +6...") directly conflict with that subset and have to be ignored on those runs. Add a one-line note: when run as the show-up subset under the orchestrator, take the email count and send timing from the deliverable spec, keep the long-form "email IS the consumable" philosophy, and make every reminder carry standalone value per emails-and-booking.md.
- The skill's send-timing checklist is hour-stamped from booking only (hour 0, +1, +3 ... -10 min), which assumes a fixed 72h runway. Real booked calls land anywhere from same-day to several days out. The skill should teach a dual anchor: value emails keyed to the booking timestamp, reminders keyed to call time, plus a compression rule for short-runway bookings. That is what actually loads cleanly into GHL.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180004 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The "No-Show Reschedule Sequence" section specs a 2-email flow (Direct Reschedule, then Opportunity Cost), but the orchestrator's Step 5 call-branch deliverable calls for a 3-email no-show recovery track (missed-you rebook, cost-of-the-unsolved-problem with a proof story, last-touch takeaway). Add a note that the flow flexes to 3 when the deliverable spec asks for it, with the third email being a standalone-value takeaway that leaves the door open to a lower-friction path (the free community) rather than another rebook push, so a non-responder is never abandoned.
- The skill has no "post-call follow-up for non-closers" section at all, yet that is half of the standard call-funnel recovery deliverable. It currently only covers pre-call (booking-to-call) and the no-show reschedule. Add a short post-call track spec (recap THEIR situation and the offer in writing, one objection reframe with proof, an honest no-chase final) so writers are not borrowing the post-webinar skill's attendee flow to fill the gap.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180004 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The skill explicitly disclaims SMS ("SMS sequences, email only") yet its voice, subject/pretext discipline, and no-show reschedule psychology are exactly what the SMS set has to mirror. A one-line pointer ("for the companion SMS set, apply these voice rules under the mother skill's Step 5 length limits") would stop the SMS writer from having to reverse-engineer the connection.
