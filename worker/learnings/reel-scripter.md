# Learnings: reel-scripter

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270003 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The opening hook must self-identify the niche and avatar in its first spoken and on-screen line, even when it reuses a market verbatim. A line that could sit on any account in any niche ("show me a check", a bare number) mistargets the reel and, in regulated categories, invites platform review. Add "name the product category, situation, or mechanism in line one" to the hook rules.
- When reel-scripter runs inside the client-onboarding-orchestrator (or any client-facing final deliverable), ship ONE final hook and ONE final CTA per reel, not the default-plus-alternates menu. The orchestrator's document rules forbid option lists and choice commentary in a document the owner hands the client; the alternates-menu is an internal ideation pass only.
- Match the CTA to the traffic's awareness level. Organic top-of-funnel reels are problem-aware at best, so the CTA must point to a low-friction next step (free community, short free training, comment keyword). A "verify me / look me up / search my name" ask belongs only on warmer, solution-aware placements and dies on cold organic.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- When a reel batch is scaled past the number of named proof wins the client actually has (here, 4 screenshot-gated wins but 5 reels requested), the extra reels must stand on the founder story or the named mechanism, never on a re-used or aggregated win. Doubling a proof screenshot across two reels breaks the one-win-per-asset discipline and reads as thin proof to a skeptical buyer. Add "if reels exceed available wins, cover the gap with story or mechanism, not a repeated screenshot" to the BANK-mode rules.
