# Learnings: reel-scripter

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270003 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The opening hook must self-identify the niche and avatar in its first spoken and on-screen line, even when it reuses a market verbatim. A line that could sit on any account in any niche ("show me a check", a bare number) mistargets the reel and, in regulated categories, invites platform review. Add "name the product category, situation, or mechanism in line one" to the hook rules.
- When reel-scripter runs inside the client-onboarding-orchestrator (or any client-facing final deliverable), ship ONE final hook and ONE final CTA per reel, not the default-plus-alternates menu. The orchestrator's document rules forbid option lists and choice commentary in a document the owner hands the client; the alternates-menu is an internal ideation pass only.
- Match the CTA to the traffic's awareness level. Organic top-of-funnel reels are problem-aware at best, so the CTA must point to a low-friction next step (free community, short free training, comment keyword). A "verify me / look me up / search my name" ask belongs only on warmer, solution-aware placements and dies on cold organic.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- When a reel batch is scaled past the number of named proof wins the client actually has (here, 4 screenshot-gated wins but 5 reels requested), the extra reels must stand on the founder story or the named mechanism, never on a re-used or aggregated win. Doubling a proof screenshot across two reels breaks the one-win-per-asset discipline and reads as thin proof to a skeptical buyer. Add "if reels exceed available wins, cover the gap with story or mechanism, not a repeated screenshot" to the BANK-mode rules.

<!-- 2026-07-17 · job 540001 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- When mining a niche where the business-teaching content and the high-reach content live on different platforms and different hook styles, report the reach paradox as the finding: if the highest-reach hook style (often story-frame transformation) is owned by consumers and absent from the business-teaching lane, that gap is the client's opening to port the high-reach shape into the under-served lane. Do not just catalog winners; flag which winning shape nobody in the client's own lane is using.

<!-- 2026-07-17 · job 570001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- When sourcing competitor outliers for a Competitor Content Intel deliverable, mine each account by blending its top performers (rank by real view count) with its newest posts, not one or the other. Top-by-views on its own surfaces stale off-niche wins (an old lyric or lifestyle clip that has nothing to do with the offer); newest on its own misses the account's proven concepts. Blend the two per account and flag any off-niche piece rather than treating raw views as a niche win.
- The deliverable is model-the-shape, never model-the-claim: carry over the hook archetype, format, and structure, and swap every competitor number, legal-code citation, offer, price, and guarantee for the client's own mechanism and screenshot-gated proof. State this explicitly in each re-skin so a downstream writer never imports a competitor's non-compliant claim by accident.

<!-- 2026-07-22 · job 780005 · client: Ibby -->
- When competitor audio cannot be transcribed, the written caption is a valid hook and CTA signal for the intel report, and it should be analyzed with the same Hook / Body / CTA split used for transcripts, flagged as caption-derived. The reach paradox (highest-reach account in the niche winning on off-niche story-frame content, not the client's topic) should be reported as a named finding whenever the top-by-views pieces are off-niche, so the client sees which winning shape nobody in their own lane is using rather than a raw view ranking.

<!-- 2026-07-22 · job 780006 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- When a reel batch is built by modeling a Competitor Content Intel doc, spread the batch across the doc's proven hook styles by its Hook-Style Leaderboard, not the one or two styles the client's own lane over-uses. In under-watched niches the business-teaching lane pumps problem-promise, data-shock and contrarian-claim while the reach actually lives in identity-call and story-frame shapes, so a batch that is all data-shock leaves the highest-reach shapes on the table. Represent the high-reach shapes, then still keep the business build on proof and mechanism.
- On a top-of-funnel reel batch spanning multiple sub-avatars of one build, allocate proof so each named client win LEADS only one reel in the batch, and vary the hook style between adjacent reels. Repeating a win or a hook style back to back reads as thin to a skeptical high-ticket buyer, the same one-win-per-asset discipline BANK mode already applies within a single reel.
