<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The bundled native-formats.md says a brand header fails the native test, but does not state the corollary plainly: phone-native formats (iMessage, Notes, search, tweet, Reminders, lock screen) carry NO brand chrome at all, and brand colours/logos belong only to the direct-response family (comparison, stat slam, marker maths, workshop, etc.). A one-line rule in the craft section would stop the recurring mistake of stamping a logo on a "screenshot" ad.
- Note that in regulated-category niches, the "state the pricing model, never the number" rule extends to on-image proof: a named client dollar result should not be banked as a bare on-image number in a format that cannot show its screenshot (e.g. a search snippet). Point the format at the receipt instead.

- The red-lines and craft-rules in references/native-formats.md should call out that a client's no-em-dash rule applies to rendered UI text, and name the sneaky slot: search-result titles, email subject lines, and tweet display text where a dash reads as "natural chrome." A comma or colon holds the format without breaking the client rule.
