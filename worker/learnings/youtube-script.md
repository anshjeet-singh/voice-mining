# Learnings: youtube-script

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270005 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The SOP is silent on regulated niches. Add a compliance gate step (run before the anti-pattern audit) for finance, health, and credit clients: no guaranteed amounts, timelines, or deletions; state the pricing MODEL never the number; proof only as [PROOF: ...] placeholders gated on a real screenshot; and any contrarian diagnosis line stays a spoken hook or body bridge, never an on-screen headline card. The universal copy-quality gates already apply, but a script writer needs them named in the skill so a regulated deliverable does not ship a claim that flags the channel.
- Add a "match the CTA to the traffic's awareness level" rule to Step 4. Cold, problem-aware YouTube traffic converts to a low-friction next step (free community, keyword lead magnet), not a booking call; a "book a call / verify me / look me up" ask belongs only on warmer, solution-aware placements and underperforms as the ask on broad organic. This mirrors the same fix already logged for reel-scripter.
- The skill offers bullets (strong-on-camera) or full draft (freezes without a script) but has no path for a founder who freezes on teleprompter AND chokes on long-form. Add a segmented long-form mode: build the video as standalone chaptered segments (hook, arcs, CTA), write the hook and CTA word for word, keep the body a beat scaffold with hero lines, and require a "freestyle from the beats, do not read verbatim, shoot in segments" recording note at the top. This resolves the same tension already promoted to a first-class WORD_FOR_WORD mode in breakout-video-writer.
- When youtube-script runs inside a client-facing deliverable (e.g. the client-onboarding-orchestrator), ship ONE final title, ONE thumbnail brief, and ONE CTA, not the "three CTA options, pick one" menu the SOP describes. The orchestrator's document rules forbid option lists and choice commentary in a document handed to the client; the options menu is an internal ideation pass only. Same carve-out already logged for reel-scripter.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270014 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The CTA rule needs a platform split. On YouTube long-form, the CTA is ALWAYS a click-the-link-in-the-description push (join the free community, watch the free training at the link), NEVER a comment-keyword ask. Comment-keyword lead magnets are an Instagram and short-form pattern; on YouTube the description link is the native low-friction destination, and a "comment KEYWORD" CTA underperforms and reads as short-form copy pasted onto a long-form asset. Step 4 currently lists "lead-magnet trigger (DM me KEYWORD on Instagram)" as a top option without flagging that it does not transfer to YouTube. Add: for YouTube specifically, default to the description-link CTA to the awareness-matched low-friction step, and reserve the comment or DM keyword for the short-form skill.
- When youtube-script runs inside a client-facing on-demand batch (e.g. the orchestrator's Content Engine), the shipped document is title, then SUMMARY, PACKAGING, RECORDING STANDARDS, STRUCTURE, SCRIPT, and nothing else, with each video its own document separated by a `<!-- SPLIT -->` line. The Step 6 anti-pattern audit still runs, but as an INTERNAL pass before delivery; it is never a section in the client-facing file. The current output-format section prints the anti-pattern audit as part of the deliverable, which is a defect for any client-facing run. Name the audit as internal-only when the deliverable is client-facing.

<!-- 2026-07-12 · job 300001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- Add the ranked tier-list (X, worst to best) as a first-class long-form format in the format-inspiration menu, and flag it as the default pick for regulated niches. It retains hard (the viewer watches to see where each rung lands), shoots cheap in segments, and can carry an entire named mechanism with ZERO proof screenshot, which sidesteps the compliance gate for finance, health, and credit clients where every stated number needs a gated receipt. The mechanism maps naturally onto the top rungs of the list.
- When a deliverable says "model the freshest Competitor Content Intel" and that intel is short-form or reel-shaped, add a step that ports the winning reel SHAPES into long-form video STRUCTURE rather than treating them as short-form-only: ranked list-tease becomes the video's spine, demo-first becomes the opening two-outcomes contrast, insider-reveal becomes the "here's what's really happening" body. The intel's per-reel angle suggestions are the raw material for the long-form arcs, not just for reels.
- The SOP's "3 to 5 story arcs" can conflict with a ranked or list format that naturally has more items than five. Add the resolution: cluster the low-value items into a single arc (the ways that hurt you), then spend the remaining arcs giving one rung each to the mechanism steps. This keeps a list video story-led instead of turning it into a stacked bullet dump, which the anti-pattern audit already forbids.
