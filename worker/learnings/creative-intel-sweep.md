# Learnings: creative-intel-sweep

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-11 · job 270007 · client: Trent Kus -->
- Instagram reels frequently download from the scraper as a video-only stream (no audio track), which makes the transcription step fail with a hard 400 and silently drops the reel. A 733K-view competitor reel was nearly lost this way. When a transcript comes back empty or the transcribe call 400s, fall back to a bestaudio download (yt-dlp) and retry before discarding the reel. High-view reels are exactly the ones worth this extra step.
- Some of the highest-reach competitor reels carry no spoken words at all (silent B-roll with a music bed); the entire ad lives in the caption plus a comment keyword. The Hook/Beat/CTA teardown must treat the caption as the copy unit for these, label it as caption-borne with a "silent B-roll, no voiceover" note, and flag the format itself as the primary lesson rather than reporting an empty transcript.
- Before scraping named competitor handles, verify each handle resolves and correct obvious typos from the intake doc; a single misspelled handle returns zero results and looks like the account has no ads. Flag any handle that returns nothing for a manual check rather than reporting the competitor as absent.
- A competitor's platform-native top reels can be entirely off-topic (motivational or lyric clips) even when that competitor is a real threat in the niche, because their sales content lives on a different platform. Report where the on-topic content actually is (Twitter, webinar ads) as a coverage gap instead of concluding the competitor is weak.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 360011 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- For Competitor Content Intel on YouTube, captions are effectively never available: the yt-scrape timedtext fetch returned no transcript for all 34 long-form videos across five channels in this run, so every YouTube read was a packaging teardown. Packaging analysis (title, description lead, chapter order, CTA destination) is the DEFAULT path for YouTube, not the fallback for the occasional caption-less video. State this so a run does not stall waiting for transcripts or under-analyze a video because its transcript is empty.
- In a thin niche the distinct business-content creator field can be smaller than the 10-source floor: here the entire peptide-business teaching field was about five creators (Aden Rao, Austin Siddiqi, Lino Aguayo, Daniel Budai, plus consumer-adjacent Nick Calabrese), who recur across every discovery search. The reliable way to reach the floor is to count each platform-account as its own source and mine the same competitors' second-platform accounts, then report the field's thinness as an intel finding (it is the client's opportunity), rather than padding the count with irrelevant consumer channels that produce zero keepers.
- A competitor's on-topic business content and their high-reach content routinely live on different platforms or accounts: peptide-business teaching on YouTube, off-topic lifestyle or unrelated-niche content (day-trading, branding, personal product use) on Instagram, where their reach actually is. Extend the existing "report where the on-topic content actually is as a coverage gap" rule to explicitly cover the YouTube-versus-Instagram split, since that split is itself the strongest positioning finding (an empty on-topic lane the client can own).
- The ElevenLabs speech-to-text step still returns a hard 400 on video-only Instagram streams and silently drops those reels (recurred on several reels this run). The known bestaudio-fallback-and-retry lesson should be promoted from a learning into the scraper's default retry path, because in a low-view niche the dropped reel can be one of the few keepers.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 420002 · client: Trent Kus -->
- A requested competitor handle that looks correct can silently resolve to a completely different account. This run's Instagram "@hunter" returned an Amazon-FBA seller ($180M Amazon sales, Amazon SEO, seller-account setup), not Hunter Tobin (who is @hxxntrr). Always compare the scraper's returned `username` field against the requested handle and discard non-matching usernames as noise rather than reporting them as the competitor's content. Prefer the exact handle from the client's onboarding doc, and when a handle returns zero results, treat it as a probable typo (here "@cheifsbs" for "@chiefsbs") and check the corrected spelling before concluding the competitor is absent.
- The Instagram scraper can surface incidental accounts the operator never requested (this run returned "smartcreditpartners" and "calma.community_" alongside the target handles, likely tags or collabs). Filter results to the requested usernames and label any extras as incidental so a stray off-niche account never gets read as a tracked competitor.
- The dedup ledger means a re-run naturally returns the newer content layer, not the account's all-time top performers (this run's ledger already held 49 previously mined reels). State in the report that the Instagram pull is the fresh layer on top of prior sweeps, so a reader never mistakes a low-view recent reel for the account's ceiling.
- The ElevenLabs speech-to-text 400 on video-only Instagram streams recurred again this run and silently drops the affected reels. This is now confirmed across multiple runs and clients; promote the bestaudio-fallback-and-retry from an accumulated learning into the ig-scrape default retry path, so a high-view keeper is never lost to a missing audio track.

<!-- 2026-07-17 · job 570001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- This skill is Foreplay/paid-ads only, but a Competitor Content Intel run on ORGANIC content (Instagram reels + long-form YouTube) reuses the same deconstruction framework cleanly: hook verbatim (first line), format, proof type, whether a mechanism is NAMED (not just tactics listed), CTA, real view counts, then cluster into angle buckets and map every bucket to the client's real proof (mark anything the client cannot honestly claim as unusable). Consider an organic sibling mode or skill so this framework is not re-derived each run.
- Organic sourcing that works when the connector is Apify + YouTube, not Foreplay: the Apify Instagram reel scraper returns per-reel videoPlayCount, likes, comments, caption, and the alt text (which captures on-screen text), and audio transcribes cleanly via a speech-to-text pass on the reel video; YouTube long-form resolves via the Data API (channels forHandle / id then playlistItems), and the video packaging (title + description + real view counts) is itself the primary intel signal for long-form even before any transcript.
- Guardrail for the transcript step: hitting YouTube's caption endpoint across many videos in one burst triggers an IP block (HTTP 429 / IpBlocked) that persists for a long cooldown and stalls the whole run. Throttle hard, or transcribe the opening hook window by pulling only the first ~3 minutes of audio (the googlevideo audio endpoint is not rate-limited the same way) and running speech-to-text on that. Also budget the speech-to-text quota, it is finite and a full sweep of 30+ reels plus 70 videos can exhaust it mid-run.

<!-- 2026-07-21 · job 690001 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- YouTube public captions came back empty for all 49 videos this run (the timedtext endpoint returns nothing). Treat packaging analysis (title, description lead, chapter order, CTA destination) as the DEFAULT path for YouTube competitor intel, not a fallback, and size the run around it. Do not block or thin a report waiting on captions that will not arrive.
- The ElevenLabs transcription key can hard-fail mid-run (401 Unauthorized), which left all 40 Instagram reels with no transcript. The intel deliverable must degrade gracefully: fall back to the caption as the copy unit, label the piece "caption only, transcript unavailable," and still ship the full analysis. Never report an empty transcript or abort the report over a transcription outage, and surface the key failure so the operator can refresh it.
- In a thin niche, the highest-reach pieces are usually OFF-core (wordless motivation, consumer transformation) while the on-niche business content is under-watched. Keep those high-reach off-core pieces in the rejects table with their real view counts and make the reach gap a headline dashboard finding (the reach paradox), rather than silently dropping them. The empty lane is the client's positioning opportunity, so it belongs in the report, not the cutting-room floor.

<!-- 2026-07-22 · job 780005 · client: Ibby -->
- The Instagram scrape can silently return zero transcripts: this run, ElevenLabs answered every speech-to-text call with a 401 (key missing the transcription permission), yet ig-scrape.py still staged all 40 reels with empty transcript fields. The competitor-intel process must probe the transcription key with one cheap call before a long run and, when it fails, fall back to caption-plus-packaging analysis (the same treatment already used for caption-less YouTube) rather than losing the whole Instagram side. State caption-mode as a supported, first-class analysis path, not a degraded one.
- ig-scrape.py only writes its batch JSON after all reels finish, and a single slow ElevenLabs call blocks up to 600 seconds, so a stalled or killed run loses every transcript held in memory. A per-reel incremental flush (append each reel to the batch file as it completes) would make truncated scrapes recoverable, matching the "write the doc incrementally" discipline the deliverable already follows.
