# Learnings: offer-extraction

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 420001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- Author the Low/Mid ticket rung of the Offers doc as a display-ready value stack (the same value-stack shape the paid Skool About page uses), not just prose, so the downstream Skool build can reproduce the offer verbatim instead of re-deriving it. Congruence across the ladder converts, and a mushy handoff forces a weak paid About page.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 420003 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- Author the Low/Mid rung of the Offers doc as a display-ready value stack in the exact paid-Skool value-stack shape (hook headline, early-bird price, proof line, mechanism, ✅ items each ending in a ($ anchor), 🎁 bonuses with ($ values), 🏷️ Total Value vs price, Book A Call CTA, 🔥 ANNUAL PLAN block, 💥 JOIN NOW), so the downstream community build reproduces the offer verbatim instead of re-deriving a weaker one. A mushy prose handoff on this rung forces a flat paid About page.

<!-- 2026-07-15 · job 420008 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The skill's Output template ("Offer Angles: 1 / 2 / 3", "Dominant Offer", "Why This, Why Now") produces an internal option list, which directly conflicts with the client-facing document rule that forbids candidates and option lists. State in the skill that the three-angle output is an intermediate working step only, and that the shipped Offers deliverable must collapse to one final ladder with the choice already made, never a ranked list of angles.
- For a foundation Offers doc, the skill should require a display-ready value stack (each line ending in a dollar anchor, summed to a total that eclipses the price) on the paid and high-ticket rungs, not prose. Prose handoffs force a weaker downstream community/sales page because the stack has to be re-derived instead of reproduced verbatim.
