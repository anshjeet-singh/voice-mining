# Learnings: meta-ad-restrictions-prep

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- This skill covers Meta's pixel/domain DATA restrictions only, but the orchestrator's ad stage instruction demands a "compliance gate" that strips quantified approval-adjacent claims, provider-performance ratios, and personal-attribute financial claims from ad COPY for regulated niches (funding, credit, finance, health). There is no ad-copy-compliance reference anywhere in the bundle to satisfy that gate. Add one: the personal-attribute rule (no "you" claim that implies knowing the viewer's credit or finances), the ban on guaranteed amounts/timelines/approvals, the ban on quantified approval-adjacent figures, and the safe reframes (capability statements, "run your own margins," real-and-screenshotted results with a "results vary" disclaimer). Without it, every writer reconstructs the same rules from client lessons.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The skill is entirely account-infrastructure and pixel-conditioning focused (restrictions, backup events, appeals, third-party attribution) and carries nothing about the ad COPY compliance that a restricted-category niche actually trips on at the creative level (quantified approval-adjacent claims, guaranteed amounts/timelines, provider-performance ratios like "nine times out of ten", stated dollar multiples). For a funding/credit/finance/health client this leaves a gap between "your account is protected" and "your creative won't get the account flagged." Add a short copy-level compliance checklist (strip guarantees, strip quantified approval ratios, replace with capability statements and "run your own numbers", competitor figures are safe, client's own price/ratio is not) so the restriction-prep skill covers the creative surface, not just the infrastructure.

<!-- 2026-07-10 · job 180009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The skill covers domain-level restrictions, pixel conditioning, and backup events thoroughly but never mentions Meta's Special Ad Categories (Credit, Employment, Housing, Social Issues). For the exact financial-services businesses this skill targets, the Credit Special Ad Category is a separate, mandatory, targeting-level constraint (no age/gender/ZIP, Special Ad Audiences only) that applies before any restriction lands. It belongs in Step 1 or Step 2 as a standing declaration requirement, not just an if-restricted contingency.
