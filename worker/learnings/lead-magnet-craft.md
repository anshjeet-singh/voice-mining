# Learnings: lead-magnet-craft

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-16 · job 510002 · client: Veda Ray -->
- The compliance section covers "no health/income guarantees," but it does not cover niches where the mechanism itself is a legal or technical INSTRUMENT (record templates, form verbiage, statute citations, trust/contract language). In those niches a lead magnet must never write the instrument, because inventing it is both a compliance risk and factually wrong. Add a rule: when the offer's mechanism includes specific legal, medical, or financial instrument language the client owns, teach the concept and the decision-making around it, but leave the exact instrument as a literal [CLIENT TO SUPPLY: ...] placeholder. This is true for every regulated-instrument niche, not this client only.
- Note the page-word-count failure mode: the 700-to-1200 bar is easy to UNDERSHOOT when a page leans on tables and callouts, because tables carry meaning in few words. State that tables and worksheets do not count toward the teaching-depth target; budget at least two substantial teaching paragraphs (the why and the how) around each table so the lesson clears 700 words of actual prose, not markup.

<!-- 2026-07-21 · job 720002 · client: Ibby -->
- The framework's contents list (item 1 "Course name, max 50 chars" and item 2 "Course description, max 500 chars") conflicts with the Stage-2 orchestrator prompt's exact per-magnet output order, which is only (a) Promise line, (b) three Pages, (c) two Worksheets, (d) Loom Script, with the name living solely in the `## Lead Magnet N:` heading and NO slot for the course description. A writer told to "output in this exact order" drops the Skool course name/description the framework requires, even though those are the two fields Skool actually needs to publish the course. This is systemic for every lead-magnet run under this orchestrator: either add the `### Course Name` (max 50) and `### Course Description` (max 500) fields to the prompt's output order, or state in the framework that when the stage prompt omits them the writer should still emit both above the Promise line.
- The "tables and worksheets do not count toward the 700-to-1200 prose target" learning is correct and load-bearing, but it bites hardest on proof/story-driven magnets (e.g. an "operator interview vault" whose pages are mostly numbers tables and side-by-side comparisons). Those pages consistently draft in at 580 to 660 prose words on the first pass and need a second teaching paragraph budgeted per table. Worth promoting into the page bar itself as a named risk for proof/case-study pages, not just a general note, so the writer front-loads two substantial "why it matters + how it works" paragraphs around each table before the first draft rather than patching after a word count.
