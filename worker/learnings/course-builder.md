# Learnings: course-builder

Accumulated craft lessons applied on every run.

<!-- 2026-07-09 · job 30001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- Template 4's depth standard ("every lesson written to the worked-example level") applied to a full multi-stage curriculum produces an unwieldy document. Add guidance to write one flagship lesson per program to full worked-example depth (the template already names "Read Your Report Like A Lender" as the exemplar) and the remaining lessons to a compact but complete structured block covering the same required fields. This keeps the deliverable usable without dropping any required field.

<!-- 2026-07-13 · job 330001 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- The MENU output format (COURSE / Promise / TIER / Module title + description / Throughline) does not match the agency's fixed Course Outline template that the mother skill actually requires: Recording Standards, a Part 1 free 5-video sales course, a Part 2 paid curriculum in Stage/Module/Lesson blocks where every lesson carries what-to-say / what-not-to-say / tool / win, and a Recording Order. A writer following only course-builder MENU produces a generic tiered menu and silently misses the required shape. Encode the fixed template (or a pointer to it) directly in the MENU output spec so the two do not diverge.
- The "what not to say" field is not just the common mistake. For regulated niches (health, finance, peptides, supplements) it must default to carrying the compliance red line for that lesson, since the outline is what the coach films from and an off-hand claim on camera is a real liability. Add a note that in regulated niches the "what not to say" field leads with the compliance guardrail.

<!-- 2026-07-13 · job 330002 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- When the client's core offer is a done-for-you build (a business, a funnel, an asset handed over) rather than a course, the paid "curriculum" is the operator playbook that ships inside the build. Do not write "how to build the thing" lessons, because the thing is already built. Structure the whole curriculum around the few jobs the operator actually owns after handoff, and open with a "you bought a build, not a course" reframe so the student stops expecting build instructions. This is true for any DFY-build client, not just this one.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 390001 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- The fixed Course Outline template requires a "Part 1 free 5-video sales course," but neither the MENU output spec nor the fixed-template note says how to STRUCTURE those five videos, so each writer improvises the arc. Encode the default shape: the five free videos map to the buyer's belief sequence (Video 1 opportunity/dream outcome, Video 2 the real problem mechanism, Video 3 the solution mechanism / Big Domino, Video 4 the vehicle plus proof / DFY-vs-DIY, Video 5 the reframe plus CTA to the workshop or call), and each free video carries goal / what-to-say / what-not-to-say / CTA. This mirrors the RMBC lead-story-problem-solution-close arc and keeps the free course a coherent sales asset, not five loose topics.
- The Course Outline needs a "Recording Order" section, and the useful default is not simply stage order. Film the Part 1 free sales course FIRST (it is the funnel's sales engine and nothing sells until it exists), then the Start Here reframe, then FRONT-LOAD the load-bearing growth stage (the stage that actually drives the client's result), then the remaining stages, ending on the stage the offer's transformation ends on. Add this priority logic to the Recording Order guidance so writers do not default to a flat 1-2-3 stage sequence.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 420001 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The agency's fixed Course Outline foundation-doc template is richer than this skill's generic MENU format and MENU mode should output it directly. Required shape: a Recording Standards block (format, length, tone, recording method, editing, "every lesson ends with a tool," and a per-video compliance rule), a Part 1 free 5-video sales course where each video carries Goal / Belief installed / CTA and video 5 makes the offer, a Part 2 paid curriculum opened by a Course Map table (Stage, what they can do after it, modules) then Stage blocks (each Module with an objective and lessons, each Lesson with Length, Objective, What to say, What not to say, Tool, The win), a Recording Order, and a Final Module Map table.
- Make the per-lesson "What not to say" line mandatory, not optional. For compliance-sensitive niches (credit, funding, health, money) it is the load-bearing part of the doc, and it is where banned methods, guarantee language, and overclaims get fenced off before anyone films.
- Mark anything that must come from the client, not be invented, with a ⚑ flag inline and collect them in a "Flagged before recording" line at the end (real sample reports, qualification thresholds, proof screenshots, existing interviews). The skill already says "flag, don't fabricate"; the template should show where the flags live.
- When the client has a locked Skool gamification ladder, map the paid-curriculum stage names onto those level names so the course and the community read as one continuous climb.

<!-- 2026-07-14 · job 420003 · client: Blake Matthews -->
- No em dashes anywhere in a Course Outline deliverable, including stage names, module titles, and lesson headings. Use a colon or restructure. This is a permanent operator preference across every client (it appeared as a hard rule in a community-doc revision but applies to all foundation docs), so the fixed Course Outline template output must never emit an em dash even in a heading like "Stage 1 — Name". Write "Stage 1: Name".
- Keep the copy vibrant, high-energy, and benefit-driven throughout the outline; do not flatten stage promises and lesson wins into neutral descriptive prose. Every stage promise and "The win" line should read like the coach talking, in their register, not like a syllabus. This is a standing operator quality bar, not a per-client taste.

<!-- 2026-07-15 · job 420008 · client: Trent Kus -->
- The MENU-mode output format in the skill (COURSE / Promise / TIER / Module / Throughline) does not match the agency's fixed Course Outline foundation-doc template used by client-onboarding-orchestrator, and silently drops required fields. When MENU runs as Step 1 Foundation doc, produce the foundation shape instead: Recording Standards block, Part 1 free 5-video sales course (each video with Goal / Belief installed / CTA, video 5 makes the offer), Part 2 paid curriculum opened by a Course Map table then Stage blocks (each Module has an objective, each Lesson carries Length / Objective / What to say / What not to say / Tool / The win), Recording Order, and a Final Module Map table. Point the skill at an existing client's Foundations exemplar before drafting.
- For compliance-sensitive niches (credit, funding, health, money) the "What not to say" line per lesson is mandatory and load-bearing, not optional. It should lead with the compliance red line (banned methods, guarantee language, overclaims) because the coach films directly from the outline and an off-hand on-camera claim is a real liability. State the safe reframe in the same breath (capability framing, "run your own numbers", pricing model not number, proof only as a screenshot-gated placeholder).
- Mark anything that must come from the client with an inline flag and collect them in a "Flagged before recording" section at the end (real sample reports, qualification thresholds, proof screenshots, missing interviews, pricing inclusions awaiting sign-off). The skill already says "flag, don't fabricate"; the template should show where the flags live.
- Where the client has a locked community gamification ladder, map the paid-curriculum stage names onto those level names so the course and community read as one continuous climb.

<!-- 2026-07-15 · job 420009 · client: Trent Kus -->
- For a coach who chokes on camera or on long-form, the Recording Order should film the coach's single most natural talking-head topic first (usually their origin or signature founder story) as a deliberate warm-up shoot, even when that lesson lives late in the curriculum. It builds on-camera confidence before the harder teaching lessons and gets usable footage in the can early. This generalizes to any camera-shy founder, not one client, so encode it as a default in the Recording Order guidance alongside "front-load the load-bearing stage."
- The per-lesson "What not to say" field should require that any compliance-banned term (for the niche) appears ONLY inside that field as a prohibition, never in the teaching copy of "What to say" or "The win." A quick final scan of the finished doc for the niche's banned vocabulary, confirming every hit sits inside a "What not to say" line, is a cheap, reliable compliance gate for regulated niches (credit, funding, health, money). Add this as a closing QA step in the MENU output spec.

<!-- 2026-07-16 · job 510001 · client: Veda Ray -->
- The existing "regulated niches" compliance guidance names health, finance, peptides, supplements, credit, funding, and money. Add a distinct category for lived-experience / legal-adjacent / spiritual-liberty niches (sovereign birth, birth-certificate and passport work, holistic health, anything "not-a-professional" personal testimony). For these, the per-lesson "What not to say" must default to TWO stacked guardrails, not one: (1) the professional-advice red line worded as "lived experience, not legal/medical/tax/financial advice, no outcome or timeline guarantee," and (2) the spiritual-or-belief-claim red line, "present the belief as my experience, never as fact or as something that produces a result." A single compliance line is not enough when the coach is simultaneously making quasi-legal how-to claims and spiritual claims in the same lesson.
- Add a note that when a client explicitly disowns an adjacent topic in their own words (here: "I don't do status correction, my lane is babies"; "I don't believe in the secret account"), that disowned topic becomes a standing prohibition across the whole outline: it appears only in "What not to say" as "refer out / do not teach," never in any "What to say" or "The win," even in lessons where it is topically nearby (e.g. the already-registered / path-to-18 module). The disown is a fence, not just a preference.
