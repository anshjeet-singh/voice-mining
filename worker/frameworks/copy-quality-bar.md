# Copy Quality Bar (applies to EVERY deliverable)

Distilled from the $100M playbooks (Offers, Hooks, Proof Checklist, Lead Nurture),
Scientific Advertising, Sell Like Crazy, and teardowns of top-converting info-industry
VSL pages. If a line of copy fails this bar, rewrite it before shipping.

## The headline bar

Headlines are governed by suby-headline-machine.md in this directory. Read it
and follow its process: 10-15 candidates across the 8 patterns, scored against
the four non-negotiables (hyper-specific dream outcome, "without" clause,
"even if" clause, curiosity device), ship the top 5-7 ranked. On top of that:

1. **Called-out avatar.** The eyebrow line above the headline is ONLY a pure
   callout: `**ATTENTION [EXACT ICP]:**`, nothing bolted on.
2. **Dream outcome, not process.** ANTI-PATTERN (never write this shape):
   "You're not getting denied because of your credit, you're getting denied
   because of how you apply." That lectures and promises nothing. The
   contrarian diagnosis works ONLY as pattern 7 in the headline machine, where
   it immediately flips into the promise.
3. **Read-aloud test.** A confident promise said in one breath, not a
   corporate slogan, not a riddle, not a parable.

## The one big idea

Every funnel asset carries exactly ONE big idea (the core promise), stated the same
way everywhere: headline, VSL, booking page, emails, ads. Congruence converts;
variation leaks. Flag the big idea at the top of the funnel core document.

## Proof beats promise

"Your promise is not a differentiator. Your proof is." Claim your proof, don't prove
your claim: start from the client's REAL results (from the foundation docs and
onboarding) and write claims those results support. Rank proof by power:
show > tell, raw > polished, recent > old, specific numbers > vague, third-party
verified > self-reported, named person > anonymous. Never fabricate proof; where the
client must insert theirs, write an explicit placeholder: `[PROOF: client result,
e.g. name + number + timeframe]`.

## Words

- Power verbs and concrete nouns. Cut: leverage, unlock, empower, elevate, journey,
  seamless, comprehensive, holistic.
- Third-grade reading level. Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Their language: pull phrases from the ICP Snapshot and research verbatims.
- Specific beats clever, clear beats cute, every time.
- No em dashes. Commas, colons, periods.

## CTAs

- One action per page/email/script. Repeated often (a VSL page repeats the same CTA
  3-5 times down the page), never varied in destination.
- Spell out the whole next step: what to click, what happens next, how long it takes,
  what they get. "Click below, pick a time, and in 15 minutes you'll know exactly how
  much funding you qualify for" beats "Learn more".
- Urgency only when real. Fake countdowns burn trust.

## Market intelligence IS the copy source

The claim payload carries the client's voice-mining research. It is not
background reading; it is the raw material. Map it section by section:

- **Pain blocks / "sounds familiar" bullets** <- painPoints and their verbatim
  examples, written as scenes
- **Headline + subheadline promises** <- top desires + buying triggers
- **FAQ questions** <- objections, phrased the way the market actually asks them
- **Fear/cost-of-inaction sections** <- fears, turned into concrete math
- **Power words and phrasing everywhere** <- emotionalLanguage + trendingPhrases
- **Testimonial specs and hooks** <- verbatimQuotes (the market talking about
  itself)
- **Positioning / "this is NOT X" enemies** <- competitor intel weaknesses + gaps

If a page section could have been written without the research, rewrite it
using the research.

## The final sense check

Read the whole asset aloud as the ICP. At any line where they would squint, stop
caring, or smell a lie, rewrite that line. Mediocre lines are defects, not filler.
