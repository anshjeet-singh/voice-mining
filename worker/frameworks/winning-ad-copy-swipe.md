## Why it wins (the anatomy to reproduce)

1. **Identity-call welcome line** — names the community AND who it's for
   ("entrepreneurs, investors, and professionals") plus the mechanism stack
   in one breath. No throat-clearing.
2. **"Inside, you'll learn" value stack** — 5 emoji-led bullets, every one a
   CONCRETE outcome with numbers where possible ($50K–$250K, 0% interest).
   Specificity over adjectives, exactly like the hook bank teaches.
3. **A "PLUS" second stack** — bonus paths with real income numbers, each
   with its own emoji identity. The stack keeps re-hooking the scroller.
4. **Value anchor + free flip** — "Total Value: $14,999 — Free Today" is the
   Hormozi value equation compressed to one line.
5. **The reframe couplet** — "This isn't just X. This is Y." elevates the
   offer from a service to infrastructure. One line each, own paragraphs.
6. **Command close** — one CTA, brand name repeated, verb-led.

## Hard rules when writing to this standard

- Same length class: 400–900 characters of real, line-broken copy. A
  caption-length one-liner is a failed ad.
- Every bullet carries a number, a timeframe, or a named mechanism.
- Emojis are signposts (one per bullet, consistent per stack), never confetti.
- The market's own words from the research fill the bullets; the STRUCTURE
  comes from here.
- End on one CTA to [COMMUNITY LINK]. Never two asks.
- Suby/Sagari/Hormozi frameworks (suby-headline-machine, hooks-and-proof,
  hormozi-offers) govern the hook line and value anchors — this file shows
  what they look like EXECUTED at winning depth.
