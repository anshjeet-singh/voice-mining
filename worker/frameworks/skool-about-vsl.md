# Skool About-Page VSL Framework (distilled from 8 winning community VSLs)

Sources studied: The Lady Change ($37/mo paid), Reta Unfiltered Inner Circle
($27/mo), LetsGetFunded Inner Circle, School of Mentors, Maker School, Blue-Collar
Biz Accelerator (testimonial VSL), Synthesizer Scaling (application VSL), and the
$350K-launch results wall. Model the SHAPES below; never reuse their sentences.

## Hard rules (from the owner)

- **Target ~3 minutes spoken** (roughly 420-480 words). Some winners run longer,
  ours do not. Every beat below still fits; compress, don't skip.
- **Gamification level names are IDENTICAL in the free and paid communities.**
  One ladder, one identity system, both communities. Derive names from the
  client's brand language (Brand & Positioning doc), 5-9 levels, ascending
  identity (novice-to-icon arc in THEIR niche vocabulary).
- Written word for word, ready to read to camera. Follow the
  vsl-and-sales-page-writer and hidden-vssl-framework skills for craft.

## The beat sequence (both community types)

1. **Callout hook** (pick one proven shape):
   - Secret/unfair-advantage: "There's something the [winners] know that you
     don't..." (Lady Change)
   - Proof bomb: open with a named member + numbers ("My name's David. I started
     at 312 pounds... down 40 pounds in eight weeks" energy) (Reta)
   - Metaphor question: "Do you know where the most valuable real estate is?
     Graveyards." (School of Mentors)
   - Direct callout: "IF YOU ARE a [avatar] who wants [outcome]..." (Synthesizer)
2. **"Does this feel familiar?" pain montage**: 3-5 second-person scenes from the
   research verbatims (wake up tired, Monday day-one-again loops, the quiet scary
   thought "maybe this is just how I am now").
3. **The terrible alternatives**: name the enemy options and why each fails
   (the wrong-fit expert, the risky shortcut, the DIY guessing). Sets up...
4. **New opportunity + named method**: "you've been playing by the wrong rules";
   reveal the client's named mechanism from Brand & Positioning.
5. **Founder authority**: the rise or the price paid, in 2-3 sentences with
   numbers ("it took me half a decade to crack the code").
6. **What's inside**: the offer stack as a fast list. For communities, daily/weekly
   call schedule BY DAY converts ("Every Monday X, every Tuesday Y") (LetsGetFunded);
   include DFY elements and direct founder access if real.
7. **Proof wall**: 3-5 member results with names and numbers, rapid-fire. If the
   client is new, use [PROOF] placeholder specs, never inventions.
8. **The close** (differs by community type, below), always ending with a fork
   ("keep doing what hasn't worked, or...") + one CTA repeated.

## Free community about VSL (~3 min)

Jobs: get the JOIN, then aim members at the pinned Start Here + booking path.
- No price section. The stack is what they get free TODAY (trainings, calls,
  community, the lead magnet).
- Close: qualification line ("this is for action takers, not shortcut hunters")
  + join CTA + tell them the FIRST action after joining (watch the pinned video,
  book the onboarding call at [BOOKING LINK]).

## Paid community about VSL (~3 min)

Jobs: sell the membership. Adds to the shared beats:
- **Value stack + anchor**: itemize what it's worth ("over $850 of proven
  programming"), then drop to the real price with a daily-cost anchor ("just
  over $1 a day, less than a cup of coffee") (Lady Change).
- **Guarantee with a name and a condition**: "Safety First 30-Day Guarantee"
  (follow the plan, feel the support, or every penny back) or Maker School's
  outcome guarantee shape (do the work, get [OUTCOME] by day 90 or refund).
  Conditions make guarantees credible.
- **Scarcity with a REASON**: price rises every N members, or "small community
  protects [the thing]" (Reta). Never fake countdowns.
- **Qualification**: one line on who this is NOT for.
- Optional application-funnel variant (high-ticket): radical results
  transparency + "we cancel over 60% of applicants" gatekeeping + the
  "if all it did was..." minimization close (Synthesizer Scaling).

## Techniques worth stealing across all of them

- Interleave 1-line member soundbites mid-VSL as pattern interrupts ("I feel
  young again... it's incredible").
- "Imagine this..." future-pacing block right before the stack: 3 concrete
  scenes of life after (slipping into your favorite jeans, waking rested).
- Blunt honesty converts: "I'm just going to list it out", time-cost honesty
  ("you'll need about two hours per day"), compliance asides said out loud.
- ROI math close for business niches: "when this helps you sign one $1,000
  deal, it has paid for itself five times."
- Callback close: end by answering the opening hook (graveyard, secret, question).
