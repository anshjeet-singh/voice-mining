Source: internet scavenge (July 2026) of the publicly documented frameworks of the named direct response masters. Hormozi's and Suby's full systems already live in their own files (hormozi-*.md, suby-*.md); this file adds Hormozi's sentence-level copy method plus the four not yet covered.

## 1. Todd Brown: The E5 Method

Five consecutive stages: Examine, Engineer, Evaluate, Enhance, Expand.

Stage 1, Examine (the research stage, "where the magic truly begins"):
- The prospect: what they think and feel, what they already know and believe about their problem, the ideal solution, your product, and competitors, and what they've always wondered about or wanted to understand.
- The product: primary promise, unique mechanism, USP, functional benefits, dimensionalized benefits, and assets for the irresistible offer.
- The competition: their promises, claims, mechanisms, positioning, and offers, plus marketplace sophistication, so your message lands as different, unique, new, fresh.

Stage 2, Engineer: build the campaign around the BIG IDEA = primary promise + unique mechanism. A big idea speaks to head AND heart: emotionally compelling (a primary promise of transformation) and intellectually interesting (that promise is fulfilled BY the unique mechanism). The Campaign Thesis Statement guides everything: the single Buying Belief that, once installed, makes the prospect see the solution as different and superior to every option. Also engineered here: the SIN offer (Superior, Irresistible, No-brainer).

Stage 3, Evaluate: turn the campaign into a minimum viable funnel (just 4 pages) and roll it out to test profitability before building anything fancy.

Stage 4, Enhance: only after proven profitable, build the minimum funnel into a full campaign.

Stage 5, Expand: scale traffic into the proven campaign.

The Lead: the first 350 to 500 words of any campaign (video, webinar, sales letter) exists to set the emotional hook by unpacking the unique mechanism and primary promise.

The Unique Mechanism: "the unique manner, method, or material that allows the product to deliver the desired benefits." In young markets, big claims work. In sophisticated markets, claims stop working and the mechanism takes over, because it gives skeptical prospects HOPE with credibility. Verbal cue: it's whatever follows "the secret is in the...". Old infomercials reveal theirs within the first 40 seconds.

## 2. Stefan Georgi: The RMBC Method

Four steps that made 12+ sales letters a month possible and cut writing time by over 80%: Research, Mechanism, Brief, Copy.

Research: 7 questions, answered from forums and Amazon reviews (5-star = what they love, 1-star = pain and horror stories):
1. Who is the customer (age, beliefs, biases, hopes, where they hang out)?
2. What are their pain points (go deep: psychological, economic, relational, not just surface)?
3. What outcome do they want, in their own words (including big-picture: respect, stability, a happy marriage)?
4. What is the market already using?
5. What do they LIKE about existing solutions?
6. What do they DISLIKE about existing solutions?
7. Are there horror stories about existing solutions?

Mechanism, two parts:
- Unique Mechanism behind the PROBLEM: the real root cause of their struggle they never knew about. This creates the "aha": past solutions failed because they addressed symptoms, not the root. It also removes self-blame.
- Unique Mechanism behind the SOLUTION: how your product addresses that true root cause, and therefore why it succeeds where everything else failed.
- Example shape (weight loss): problem mechanism = a protein deficiency in fat tissue blocks metabolism; solution mechanism = the product reactivates that protein.

Brief: 12 questions synthesizing research into pre-written blocks. Key ones: who's the audience, the 1 to 3 main pains and fears, the big promise in 1 to 3 sentences, what solutions exist and why they don't work, the full unique mechanism written out, the product's features and benefits, the background story plus story of discovery (spokesperson credibility and how they found the mechanism). CRITICAL RULE: write brief answers as if they're going into the actual sales letter, not as bullets. The brief becomes paste-ready copy: this is where the 80% time saving lives.

Copy: a fixed modular sequence (53 elements, majors below). Order matters, don't rearrange:
1. Lead: rapport, curiosity, the big promise against the primary pain.
2. Background story: spokesperson credibility, the discovery narrative.
3. Unique mechanism behind the problem.
4. Unique mechanism behind the solution.
5. Product build-up and reveal: why nobody else offers this.
6. Close: name, features, benefits, emotional picture of life with it, comparison to alternatives, price reveal, explicit CTA.
7. FAQ: objection handling, worth an extra 10 to 15% conversion on its own.

Applies beyond sales letters: emails, Facebook and YouTube ads, headlines, subject lines, advertorials.

## 3. Dan Henry: Sell Before You Create

The Sold-Out Courses blueprint ($100k in month one by PRE-selling):
1. Create a Facebook group around the skill or topic.
2. Provide value for 5 days, build to 200+ members.
3. SURVEY the members to find what to teach (the market names the course, not you).
4. Build a basic webinar deck over 7 to 8 days.
5. Promote the webinar for 3 days with daily content previews.
6. Host it live and sell EARLY-BIRD access to the not-yet-built course with a money-back guarantee. Build the course with the buyers inside.

Offer clarity statement: desire + roadblock + method, WITHOUT mentioning the product. The marketing statement sells the transformation route, the product is just the vehicle.

Quick Win Syndrome: engineer a quick win DURING the webinar (a script or asset they use while still watching) so the audience experiences the future before being asked to buy. Someone who just won with your free material extrapolates the paid material.

Scale path: paid traffic into the proven webinar, prioritize upsells and cross-sells, heavy customer support and education (support IS marketing at high ticket), continuous funnel testing. Tiny behind-the-scenes webinar optimizations took his automated webinar to $2.2M in 11 months.

## 4. Jason Wojo: The Offer Is the Engine

Diagnostic order when ads underperform (fix in this order, never backwards): offer > messaging > creative > landing page > channel mix.

- The offer is the engine, creative only sets the tone. Nail the offer and everything downstream gets easier to optimize.
- Testing IS the strategy. The boldest hook in the world loses to systematic testing of hooks, angles, and conversion paths. Scale = volume of tests plus continuous refinement.
- Ad teardown checklist: first 3 seconds, headline or hook, proof structure, the offer, the landing page handoff, the follow-up sequence that carries the conversion.
- Back end is where the real money prints: nurture flows, strategic upsells, checkout psychology, frictionless paths. When the front end can't carry the weight, the back end more than makes up for it.
- Front-end setups: risk-reversal guarantees, low-cost trip wires framed against higher anchors (shipping-only book offers), capturing payment info early to reduce friction on the next ask.
- Every channel (Meta, TikTok, Google, YouTube) is one connected buying path: ads, scripts, landing pages, and follow-up all carry the SAME core promise.
- Positioning: you don't need to be everything to everyone, be undeniable to the right audience. Clarity and polarity separate prospects.

## 5. Hormozi's Own Copy Method: Pain Is the Pitch

(His offer/leads/closing systems are in the hormozi-*.md files. This is how he writes sentences. He writes his own copy; there is no hidden ghostwriter.)

- Pain beats promise. Motivation is deprivation. The reader's logic: "if this guy knows this much about my pain, he has to know how to solve it." Ratio: 80% of the copy articulates the problem, 20% the solution.
- Observable Reality Rule: no sentence a court witness couldn't verify. Banned: trust, energy, "we care about your success." Replace with observables: "we host 3-hour weekly strategy calls to review your data," "dashboard loads in under 400 milliseconds," "99.9% uptime since 2018."
- No persuasion occurs in the vague. Find the vivid PHYSICAL manifestations of the pain: not "overweight" but "held your breath 30 seconds to button your jeans," "ducked out of photos," "always the beach cover-up."
- Rhetoric toolbox: opposites ("risk looking broke to get rich"), sets of three ("do work tired, do work nervous, do work imperfectly"), alliteration for framework names, short syntax (zero thinking required to parse).
- The Google Doc test: a plain doc with an Eventbrite link did $50k off $5k ad spend while the designed landing page for the same product did near zero. If the words don't convert on a white background, design cannot save them. Test copy text-only before investing in production.
- Implementation: list 3 visceral physical pains, rewrite headlines with opposites or triplets, purge sentimental jargon for observables, test in plain text first.

## Where This Maps in Our Pipeline

- Market research and foundation docs: Georgi's 7 research questions are the interview and mining script (forums, Amazon reviews, sales calls). Brown's Examine stage adds the competitor sophistication audit: catalog every competitor promise and mechanism so ours reads new.
- Offer docs: every client offer needs a NAMED unique mechanism, both halves (problem mechanism + solution mechanism). If the offer doc can't complete "the secret is in the...", the mechanism work isn't done. Campaign Thesis Statement (the one Buying Belief) goes at the top of every foundation doc.
- VSL and webinar scripts: RMBC's copy sequence is the skeleton (lead > story > problem mechanism > solution mechanism > build-up > close > FAQ). Brown's rule: the first 350 to 500 words exist only to set the emotional hook via promise + mechanism. Henry's Quick Win goes mid-webinar: give them something they use before the pitch.
- Funnel build order: Brown's Evaluate stage = ship the 4-page minimum viable funnel first, prove profit, THEN enhance. Never build the full campaign before the market votes.
- New offers for clients: Henry's pre-sell blueprint (group > value > survey > webinar > early-bird) is the zero-risk launch path for any client's first digital product.
- Ads and diagnosis: Wojo's fix order (offer > messaging > creative > landing page > channel) is the triage sequence when a client's campaign stalls. Never blame creative before auditing the offer.
- Copy QA (all deliverables): Hormozi's three tests are mandatory: 80/20 pain-to-solution ratio, Observable Reality Rule (no unverifiable sentence), Google Doc test (would these words sell on a plain page?). The RMBC brief rule applies to our research docs: write findings as paste-ready copy sentences, not bullets, so downstream deliverables assemble instead of restart.
- Emails: RMBC applies directly. Anderson's discipline: rotate new angles monthly per client, on the calendar, not when inspiration strikes.
