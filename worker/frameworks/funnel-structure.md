# Funnel Structure Spec (page-by-page anatomy)

BEFORE writing any funnel page, read vsl-swipe-file.md in this directory: 12
broken-down cold-traffic funnels from the top of the info industry. Model the 2-3
closest to the client's market. The anatomy below is the default skeleton; the
swipe file shows how winners flesh it out.

The Funnel Structure document must contain the COMPLETE copy for every page, section
by section in page order, so the owner can build the page in any builder without
writing a word. For each section: the copy itself, plus a one-line layout note in
brackets. Anatomy synthesized from the $100M playbooks and current top-converting
info-industry VSL/webinar pages.

## Page 1 of a call funnel: the VSL page

Single column, one CTA repeated. Sections in order:

1. **Eyebrow line**: `**ATTENTION [EXACT ICP]:**` in small caps above the headline.
2. **Headline**: passes the headline bar (see copy-quality-bar.md). The single
   biggest promise the proof can back.
3. **Subheadline**: expands the promise with the mechanism and kills the #1
   objection. "Watch the short video below to see how [MECHANISM] gets [AVATAR]
   [RESULT] in [TIME], without [OBJECTION]."
4. **The VSL embed** + a caption line commanding them to watch ("Watch this before
   you book. It answers the 5 questions everyone asks.").
5. **Primary CTA button** directly under the video: outcome-worded button text
   ("Get My Funding Plan" beats "Book a Call") + one reassurance line under it
   (free, 15 minutes, no obligation).
6. **Proof strip**: numbers row (clients served, $ results, years, ratings) with
   `[PROOF: ...]` placeholders where the client supplies real numbers.
7. **"Sounds familiar" pain block**: 3-5 bullets in the market's verbatim language
   (pull from research). Each bullet is a scene, not an abstraction.
8. **The mechanism reveal**: name the one-of-one mechanism from Brand & Positioning,
   3 steps max, each step one sentence of what happens and what it gets them.
9. **Offer stack summary**: what they get on/after the call, stacked as bullets with
   the dream outcome per item.
10. **Testimonial block**: 2-3 `[PROOF]` slots, each specced as name + specific
    number + timeframe + one-line quote.
11. **Second CTA** (same button, new supporting line addressing hesitation).
12. **Risk reversal / qualifier**: who this is for, who it is NOT for. Qualifying
    copy raises show quality.
13. **FAQ accordion**: the market's top 5 REAL objections (from research) answered
    in 2-3 sentences each, each answer ending by pointing back to the CTA.
14. **Final CTA** with a cost-of-inaction line (what staying stuck costs in numbers).

## Page 2 of a call funnel: the booking page

1. **Header confirms they're in the right place**: `**ATTENTION [ICP]**` +
   headline telling them the next step and why: "You're one step away. Pick a time
   below and [PAYOFF OF THE CALL]."
2. **Calendar embed rules**: time slots visible immediately on load (mobile and
   desktop), maximum 3 days out shown (closer bookings show up at much higher
   rates), as few form fields as possible, never re-ask anything already collected.
3. **Under the calendar**: 2-3 bullets on exactly what happens on the call, one
   short `[PROOF]` testimonial, and a photo/one-liner of a real human they'll
   talk to (faceless booking pages book worse).
4. **After booking**: confirmation page copy pointing at the call-confirmed video +
   command to watch it now + calendar-add instruction.

## Webinar funnel pages

1. **Registration page**: eyebrow callout, headline promising the ONE big teaching
   outcome + date/time, subhead with the 3 secrets bulleted (curiosity, no how-to),
   registration form (fewest fields), proof strip, presenter credibility block
   (the rise or the price paid), final CTA.
2. **Thank-you page**: confirms registration, commands calendar-add, plays the
   thank-you video, and gives ONE homework action that raises show-up (join the
   community via [COMMUNITY LINK], watch a short pre-training).
3. **Replay page**: headline with deadline, the recording, offer stack recap,
   CTA to [BOOKING LINK], countdown only if the deadline is real.

## Placeholders

Use `[BOOKING LINK]`, `[VSL LINK]`, `[COMMUNITY LINK]`, and `[PROOF: ...]`
everywhere a real URL or client asset goes. Never invent URLs, testimonials,
or numbers the foundation docs don't contain.
