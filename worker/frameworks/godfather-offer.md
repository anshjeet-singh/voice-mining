# The Godfather Offer (Sell Like Crazy Phase 4, mined line by line)

"A compelling offer is infinitely more powerful than a convincing argument."
The offer is the tip of the spear: a strong offer succeeds in spite of weak
copy, but strong copy never overcomes a weak offer. Test for every offer we
write: **if it wouldn't keep the founder up at night, it's not strong enough.**
The reader should think "how can they possibly offer this?"

## Worked examples of the calibre required

- Casper: "Get America's best-reviewed mattress, delivered to your door for
  free, for a 120-night trial." (took a startup to $600M in 4 years)
- Enso Homes: "We'll build your new home in just 30 weeks or give you $5,000
  in cash." (built off ONE researched pain: builders dragging timelines while
  buyers pay rent + mortgage; zero to $7M in 8 months)
- King Kong: "Guaranteed Google rankings in 90 days or we work for free."
  (built off the research insight that 2/3 of prospects were burned by a
  previous agency)
- The pattern: the offer is BUILT FROM the market's #1 researched pain, states
  a specific outcome + timeframe, and carries the risk itself.

## Weak offers (never ship these as the value proposition)

Great customer service / outstanding quality / innovative / great team /
responsive / great reputation. And never vague verbs: "explore our designs",
"best-in-class experts". If the offer line could sit on any competitor's
site, it is not an offer.

## The seven parts (build every offer section with all seven)

1. **Rationale**: a credible WHY for the generosity (introductory offer,
   efficiency passed on, advocate-vs-shared-enemy positioning). An
   unexplained outrageous offer reads as a scam.
2. **Build value**: establish the real everyday price with forensic proof
   (where it sells at full price, how many paid it), sell why it's worth it
   even at full price, THEN reveal the discount as the champion's move.
   Reduce to the ridiculous: "$1.33 a day, less than the coffee you didn't
   think twice about this morning."
3. **Pricing**: lead with the most aggressive low-friction entry (free named
   session, loss-leader) and save choices for later; every extra front-end
   option kills sales. For coaches/consultants the Godfather offer IS a free
   30/45/60-minute named session ('Control Your Destiny' Session, valued at
   $997), and you still SELL it hard: attach a dollar value, name it, stack
   what they leave with.
4. **Payment options**: break high prices into 3-4 payments; today-money
   matters more than tomorrow-money.
5. **Premiums**: bonuses double or triple response. Aligned to the offer
   (the funding client gets a 'Bank-Ready File Checklist', not steak knives).
   Name each premium, give it a value, stack them.
6. **Power guarantee** (build with the 7 steps):
   study competitors' guarantees -> anchor on your provable strength -> make
   it SPECIFIC and measurable ("follow the program and get [outcome] in
   [days] or every cent back", never vague "satisfaction") -> choose a
   memorable payback -> test variants (longer guarantees pull harder; <5% of
   customers ever claim, honest buyers outnumber abusers 5:1) -> put it front
   and center on every page -> NAME it ("Safety First 30-Day Guarantee",
   "Take It To The Bank Guarantee", "110% Call-Me-Crazy Guarantee").
   The guarantee is a PROOF element: it demonstrates confidence.
7. **Scarcity with a reason**: expiry date, price rise at N members, limited
   capacity ("we only have so many hours in a day"). Genuine or nothing;
   fake countdowns burn the brand.

## Process

1. Detail Sheet first: every feature in column one, translated into benefits
   (and benefits-of-benefits: the latex mattress becomes deeper sleep becomes
   the promotion) in column two. Offers write themselves from this sheet.
2. Write the magic-wand version of the offer (no rules), then dial back to
   the strongest promise the client can actually deliver.
3. List the top 3 objections to the offer and answer each inside the offer
   copy itself.
4. Final pass: "what can I add to make this even MORE compelling?" then edit
   ruthlessly: what they get, when they get it, why it benefits them, zero
   confusion.
