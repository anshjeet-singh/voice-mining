# Emails + Booking Behavior ($100M Lead Nurture applied)

## Why the 14-day community sequence exists

The free Skool community is the lead pool. The sequence's ONE job: get the joiner
to book (and show up to) the onboarding call. "If they do not show, they cannot buy."

## The four show-rate pillars (bake into the copy and the asks)

1. **Availability**: always point at the scheduler with visible time slots.
   Copy should imply abundance of times, not scarcity of access.
2. **Speed**: leads contacted within the first minutes convert several times
   better (contact within 60 seconds correlates with ~4x conversion; most of the
   market waits days). Email 1 goes out INSTANTLY on join, and the sequence
   front-loads: days 1-3 carry the hardest booking pushes.
3. **Personalization**: reference what they actually did (joined the community,
   watched the pinned video, their niche). Write like one human texting another.
4. **Volume**: most sellers quit after ~1 attempt; winners follow up 5+ times.
   Fourteen emails is the feature, not the bug. Vary the angle, never the ask.

## Booking mechanics the copy must respect

- Push scheduling within 3 days ("grab a time this week" energy). Appointments
  booked closer show up far more.
- One CTA per email: [BOOKING LINK]. The community and VSL support the ask,
  they never replace it.
- Every email is skimmable in 20 seconds: short lines, one idea, one ask.

## 14-day arc (angles, one per day)

1. Welcome + instant win + soft CTA (what to do first in the community, then
   "want the fast path? book here").
2. The big idea (core promise from the funnel, in one story).
3. Client proof story #1 (raw, specific numbers, [PROOF] spec).
4. The mechanism explained (what makes this different, no how-to).
5. Objection crusher #1 (the market's top objection from research).
6. Cost of inaction (what 12 more months of stuck costs, in their numbers).
7. Client proof story #2 (different sub-avatar than #3).
8. FAQ / "what happens on the call" (kills call anxiety, restates zero risk).
9. Objection crusher #2.
10. The founder's story (the price paid, epiphany-bridge shape: struggle,
    discovery, result, bridge to them).
11. Community wins roundup (social proof in motion, point at pinned posts).
12. Direct ask + honest scarcity (calendar fills, spots this week).
13. Last-chance framing for THIS sequence (not fake: the emails slow down after).
14. Open-loop close: door stays open, tell them exactly how to re-engage, final
    CTA.

Subject lines follow the hook bank (statements and commands win; curiosity
without clickbait; 2-6 words; lowercase-casual fits the community voice).
Whisper-tease-shout pacing across the arc: hint early, build mid, direct
push late.

## SMS set rules

Booking confirmation, pre-call asset nudge (pre-call hub link), 24h / 3h / 15min
reminders, post-no-show rebook. Under 320 characters, first name, contractions,
one link, one CTA, no emoji unless the client's own texting voice uses them.
The 15-minute reminder is the highest-leverage text: one line, zero friction
("see you in 15, [NAME]. link: [CALL LINK]").
