# Sell The Click: Ad Writing (Sell Like Crazy Phase 5, mined line by line)

The ad's ONLY job is to sell the click. Not the product, not the brand, not
the booking: the click. Every ad we write (Facebook, Google, YouTube pre-roll
scripts, ad batches) is judged on one axis: does it make NOT clicking feel
expensive?

## The click drivers (pick 1-2 per ad, never zero)

Curiosity (the strongest), shock, direct benefit, implied benefit, fear,
vanity, self-interest. Curiosity + self-interest is the workhorse combo:
tease an outcome they want and withhold the mechanism.

## Google / search ads

- Write a MINIMUM of 20 headline attempts per ad. Then build
  Franken-headlines: stitch the strongest fragments of the losers into new
  candidates. The winner is assembled, not born.
- Match the searcher's intent word for word: mirror the query in the headline.
- Say something DIFFERENT from the other ads on the page. Scan what
  competitors' ads say, then take the opposite lane (they scream "cheapest",
  we scream "guaranteed outcome + timeframe").
- Every ad: benefit-loaded headline, proof or specificity in the description,
  one clear action.

## Facebook / native feed ads (the news-style pattern)

Facebook is Tinder traffic: they were interrupted, not searching. The ad must
look NATIVE to the feed, like a story a friend shared, not an ad:

- **Link headline**: 12-18 words / roughly 80-95 characters, written like a
  news or magazine story headline, not a slogan. Intrigue + benefit.
- **Primary text**: opens with a pattern interrupt or a bold claim, agitates
  the pain in their own words, teases the mechanism, commands the click.
- **Image/creative**: NO text-heavy images. Scroll-stopping, native-feeling
  photos beat designed graphics. The image buys the eyeball; the copy sells
  the click.
- **CTA button**: "Learn More" outperforms hard-sell buttons on cold traffic.
- Never sell the meeting/product in the ad. Sell the free value (the HVCO,
  the training, the case study). Cold traffic + marriage proposal = death.

## The Larger Market Formula (who the ad speaks to)

3% buying now, 17% gathering information, 20% problem-aware but not looking,
60% not even problem-aware. Ads written only for the 3% (direct "book a call"
offers) fight everyone over the smallest slice at the highest CPMs. Value-led
ads (HVCO, training, checklist) harvest the 37% nobody else is talking to.
Write ad sets for BOTH: direct-response for the 3%, value-first for the rest.

## Ad copy checklist (run every ad against this)

1. Would this stop the exact ICP mid-scroll? (pattern interrupt present?)
2. Is at least one click driver deployed? (curiosity/fear/benefit/etc.)
3. Does it read native to the platform, not like an ad?
4. Is the click's payoff crystal clear and valuable BEFORE any commitment?
5. Zero unsupported hype; specificity and proof carry the claim
6. One command, one destination

## Where this maps in our pipeline

- **Ad batches deliverable**: 20-headline minimum + Franken-headline pass is
  the mandatory process; ship ranked finals only.
- **Funnel copy**: the ad and the landing page headline must be congruent
  (same big idea, same promise) or the click is wasted.
- **Video scripts (10 numbered)**: each script's hook = a click driver; the
  first 3 seconds are the ad's headline.
