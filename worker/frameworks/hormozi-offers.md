# Hormozi $100M Offers (mined cover to cover)

The offer is the strategy layer UNDER every funnel, community, and email we
write. Suby's 17 steps sell the offer; this file is how the offer itself gets
built and enhanced. Use it when writing offer docs, paid-community pitches,
Godfather offer sections, bonus stacks, guarantees, and program names.

## Market first: the starving crowd

Starving Crowd (market) > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills. A great market
forgives everything; a dying market defeats everything. Four indicators when
sizing a client's niche: (1) massive PAIN ("the pain is the pitch": articulate
their pain accurately and they almost always buy), (2) purchasing power,
(3) easy to target (associations, lists, groups, channels), (4) growing.
All markets reduce to health, wealth, relationships. Niche down inside one:
riches are in the niches, and the SAME product is worth 100x more when named
for one avatar ("Time Management" $19 vs "Time Management For Outbound B2B
Power Tools & Gardening Sales Reps" $1,997). Never write generic-avatar copy.

Pro tip worth tattooing: "The point of good writing is for the reader to
understand. The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel
UNDERSTOOD."

## Premium pricing (the virtuous cycle)

Never price against the market average; that is pricing for market efficiency
and razor-thin survival. Price so high it creates a category of one ("this is
so much more expensive, there must be something entirely different going on
here"). Raising price literally raises perceived value (wine study) and client
results (investment = attention = adherence). Lowering price decreases client
emotional investment, perceived value, results, and margin all at once. Copy
implication: we never apologise for price; we build value until price feels
like a steal ("$100,000 of value for $10,000: money at a discount").

## The Value Equation (the four levers every claim must pull)

VALUE = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
        / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)

- **Dream outcome** (increase): sell the vacation, not the plane flight.
  Status is the deep driver; frame benefits as how OTHERS will perceive them
  ("your golf buddies' jaws will drop... only you will know what changed").
- **Perceived likelihood** (increase): people pay for certainty. Proof,
  track record, guarantees, "10,000th patient vs first patient".
- **Time delay** (decrease): fast beats free. Engineer and SELL the early win
  (first sale in 7 days, live in 14 days) because the long-term outcome is
  what they buy but the short-term win is why they stay.
- **Effort & sacrifice** (decrease): DFY beats DIY at every price. List what
  it "costs" them beyond money and delete those costs in the copy
  (liposuction vs fitness: "fall asleep, wake up thin").

The GOAL is the bottom of the equation at zero. Perception is reality: the
London tube dotted map beat faster trains. State every offer claim so it
moves at least one lever, and audit finished copy against all four.

## Building the Grand Slam Offer (5 steps; use for offer docs)

1. **Dream outcome**: the destination experience, with a number + timeframe.
2. **List ALL problems**: everything before, during, and after each step of
   getting the result. Each problem has 4 flavors (not worth it / won't work
   for me / too hard / too slow) mirroring the value equation. 16 core
   problems with sub-problems is normal. More problems = more value to sell.
3. **Solutions list**: reverse every problem into a "How to ___ even if ___"
   line. Solve EVERY problem: one unsolved "but what about eating out?"
   kills the sale.
4. **Delivery vehicles** (the Delivery Cube): for each solution pick
   1-on-1 / small group / one-to-many; DIY / DWY / DFY; live medium or
   recording format; response speed. The 10x / 1/10th test: what would you
   deliver at 10x the price? What still works at 1/10th?
5. **Trim & stack**: cut high-cost low-value, keep low-cost high-value
   (one-to-many assets: templates, calculators, systems) plus high-cost
   high-value only where it wows. Present as a named stack: each element
   gets Problem -> Solution wording -> sexier bundle name -> dollar value
   ("Foolproof Bargain Grocery System, $1,000 value"), then total the stack
   against the price. The bundle must solve ALL problems, feel one of a kind,
   and be impossible to compare to the guy down the street.

## Scarcity (function of quantity)

Fear of loss beats desire for gain. Three types: limited seats/slots, limited
bonuses, never-available-again. Service patterns that fit our clients:
total business cap ("only 25 clients, period"), growth-rate cap ("5 new
clients a week, 3 spots gone"), cohort cap ("doors open 4x a year").
Honest scarcity is the easiest: state real capacity ("81% full this week").
ALWAYS sell out, always announce the sellout (social proof + pent-up demand
for next open). Hormozi Law: the longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask
you can make. Free lead magnets get scarcity too ("only 20 downloads a week").

## Urgency (function of time)

Four ethical patterns: (1) cohort-based rolling urgency ("sign up today,
start Monday, or wait for the next kickoff"), (2) rolling seasonal promos
with REAL dates and a new name per season (New Year promo -> Valentines
promo: same offer, new wrapper, real deadline), (3) pricing/bonus-based
urgency (the discount or bonus expires, not the service), (4) exploding
opportunity (the arbitrage window itself is closing). Deadlines drive
decisions; 50-60% of launch sales land in the last 4 hours. Never raise
prices without announcing "price goes up on X": integrity + cash influx.

## Bonuses (the splinter stack)

A single offer is LESS valuable than the same offer broken into named parts
and stacked. Rules for every bonus we write:
- Special name with a benefit in the title; price tag ascribed and justified
- Tell them: how it relates to their problem, what it is, how you
  discovered/built it, how it makes their life faster/easier/lighter
- Proof it works, then a vivid image of life WITH it already working
- Tools, checklists, templates, swipe files > extra trainings (lower effort
  = higher value equation score)
- Each bonus kills one specific objection/obstacle in their head
- Solve their NEXT logical problem before they even hit it
- Total bonus value should ECLIPSE the core offer value
- Add scarcity/urgency to bonuses themselves ("3 tickets left", "today only")
- 1-on-1 selling: ask for the sale FIRST, then reveal bonuses (wow moment);
  if they say no, add the bonus matching their obstacle and ask again
  (reciprocity closes)
- Advanced: partner businesses supply free products/services as bonuses
  (free marketing for them, real value for us, affiliate revenue both ways)
- Never discount the core offer to close; add bonuses instead. Discounting
  teaches customers your prices are negotiable.

## Guarantees (risk reversal; conversion 2-4x)

The #1 objection is always risk. Power comes from the conditional statement:
"If you don't get X in Y time, we will Z." Without the "or what", it's
wishy-washy noise. Four types, stackable:
1. **Unconditional**: no questions asked refund (or refund + costs, or
   refund + $1,000). Strongest pull, riskiest; best low-ticket B2C.
2. **Conditional** ("better than money back"): outsized refund (double money
   back, "I'll buy your store for $25,000"), service guarantee (we keep
   working free until X: nobody sane claims it), modified service (free
   extra Y months), credit-based, personal service (1-on-1 until result,
   with conditions), ancillary-cost (hotel + airfare), wage-payment,
   release-of-contract, delayed second payment (no second bill until first
   outcome), first-outcome (we pay ad spend until first sale).
3. **Anti-guarantee**: "all sales are final" with a bold reason why (massive
   exposure of proprietary IP). Owns the position, filters buyers.
4. **Implied**: performance/revshare/profit-share deals; only paid if it
   works.
Higher ticket + more business-focused = more specific/conditional. Do the
math, not the emotion: 130 sales with 10% refunds beats 100 sales with 5%.
Tie conditions to the actions that make clients succeed. NAME the guarantee
with imagery ("Club a Baby Seal Guarantee") and stack unconditional 30-day
on top of conditional triple-money 90-day for maximum teeth.

## Naming: the M-A-G-I-C formula

Offers fatigue; the wrapper gets renamed, never the machine. Name = 3 to 5 of:
- **M**agnetic reason why (Free, 88% Off, Grand Opening, New Year)
- **A**vatar called out, hyper-specific and local where relevant
  (Lakeway Moms, not Austin Moms)
- **G**oal: the dream outcome in a phrase (Pain Free, 6-Figure, First Client)
- **I**nterval: timeframe (21-Day, 6-Week) - drop when compliance forbids
  quantified claim + duration
- **C**ontainer word: Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Masterclass,
  Accelerator, System, Sprint, Reset, Detox, Fast Track, Game Plan
Rhyme or alliterate when it comes free (Six-Pack Fast Track). Name every
stack item and bonus with the same formula.

When response decays, vary in THIS order (cheapest first): creative -> body
copy -> headline/wrapper -> duration -> free/discount enhancer -> monetization
structure. Never rebuild the offer that's converting; rinse and repeat.

## Where this maps in our pipeline

- **Foundation offer doc**: 5-step build (dream outcome from research desires,
  problems from painPoints, solutions, delivery cube, trim & stack with named
  + valued items).
- **Funnel copy**: value equation audit on every promise; stack + price
  reveal; named guarantee; honest scarcity/urgency with reasons.
- **Skool paid-community pitch**: bonus splinter stack; cohort urgency.
- **Emails**: bonus reveals, deadline mechanics, price-rise announcements.
- **Program names / community names / lead magnets**: MAGIC formula.
