# Hooks + Proof (for headlines, video scripts, emails, ads)

## Hook anatomy ($100M Hooks)

Every hook has two parts:
1. **The call-out**: makes the right person think "this is for me" (their label,
   their pain, their situation, their name for themselves). The cocktail-party
   effect: one specific signal cuts through all the noise.
2. **The value condition**: if [TARGET] does [CONDITION], they get [VALUE].
   Explicit or implied, it must be present. A hook with no value promise is noise.

## The 8 hook types (with pattern skeletons, adapt with client specifics)

Ranked by share of Hormozi's 121 all-time winners: statements (~48%) and
commands (~22%) dominate; questions, exclamations, lists, conditionals,
narratives fill the rest.

1. **Statement**: "The smartest thing a [AVATAR] can do in [YEAR] is..."
2. **Command**: "Read this if you're tired of [PAIN]." / "Stop [COMMON ACTION]."
3. **Label call-out**: "[AVATAR]s: I have something for you."
4. **Yes question**: "Would you pay [SMALL PRICE] to get [DREAM] in [TIME]?"
5. **Open question**: "Which would you rather be: [BAD STATE] or [GOOD STATE]?"
6. **Conditional**: "If [SITUATION], you're [DIAGNOSIS], and here's the fix."
7. **List**: "The [N] reasons [AVATAR]s stay [STUCK STATE]."
8. **Narrative**: open inside a real scene with stakes ("The sixth time I was
   arrested..." energy: specific, personal, mid-action).

Rules: numbers beat adjectives; specific personal details beat generic claims;
the first sentence exists to make them consume the second; never spend the hook
making the SPEAKER feel important, make the VIEWER feel seen (the Larry King
lesson: the re-shot hook that asked "have you ever looked in the mirror and
thought I should be further ahead by now?" turned a bombed infomercial into a
monster seller by changing only the opening sentences).

## 70-20-10 for variants

When producing hook/headline variants: 70% modeled on proven winners (the client's
own best performers, the niche's outliers, this bank), 20% winner-adjacent (a
pattern proven in another niche, adapted), 10% wild swings.

## Proof checklist ($100M Proof)

Proof is data supporting a claim. The more compelling the claim, the more proof it
needs. When writing any proof element, push it up these gradients:

in-person > virtual | live > recorded | raw > processed | show > tell |
others saying it > you saying it | opposite of competitors > identical |
personal > generic | big results > small > none | newer > older | more > less |
third-party verified > unverified | with numbers > without | metaphor > jargon

Application in copy:
- Every claim gets a proof neighbor: claim line, then proof line.
- Write proof specs the client fills: `[PROOF: raw phone-video testimonial,
  client name, starting point -> result, timeframe]`.
- Belief is a continuum from doubt to certainty; every section of a page or
  script should move the reader one notch toward certainty. Cut anything that
  doesn't.
