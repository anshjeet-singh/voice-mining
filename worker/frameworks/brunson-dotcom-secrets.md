# Brunson DotCom Secrets Playbook (mined cover to cover, all 13 secrets + 7 funnels)

The funnel-architecture half of the canon. Hormozi governs the offer and the
ads; Suby governs the copy voice; this governs the STRUCTURE: what pages
exist, in what order, what script runs on each, and how a stranger ascends
from free to high ticket. Every funnel we ship for a client maps to one of
the seven funnels here.

## The Secret Formula (upstream of every funnel)

1. WHO is your dream client?
2. WHERE are they congregating?
3. What BAIT pulls them out (they will always be attracted to the bait)?
4. What RESULT do you want to give them (the highest level of service)?
The bait must match the result: change the bait, change the customer.

## The Value Ladder and the funnel

- Value Ladder: bait (free) -> frontend ($7-$97) -> middle ($100-$2k) ->
  backend ($2k+) -> continuity somewhere in the ladder ("if you don't have
  continuity, you don't have a business").
- A funnel is the ONLINE version of the ladder: it provides value at every
  price level AND identifies who can afford the top.
- Dan Kennedy: "Ultimately, the business that can spend the most to acquire
  a customer wins." Deeper ladder = higher LTV = outspend everyone.
- List math: expect roughly $1 per name per month when the communication
  funnel is run right. The only goal of ALL traffic is to own it.

## Three types of traffic

1. **Traffic you own** (email list, followers): the only asset. Everything
   converts INTO this.
2. **Traffic you control** (paid): send it to a squeeze page with ONE
   choice: opt in or leave.
3. **Traffic you don't control** (SEO, social, mentions): send it to
   content whose top third is a glorified squeeze page.

## The Attractive Character (the voice of every email and VSL)

Four elements:
1. **Backstory**: they follow you because they were where the reader is.
   The AC does not have to be the founder: a client success story works
   (Subway used Jared, not the owner).
2. **Parables**: teach every principle through a small story (Coach took my
   wallet: "if I gave you the tape free you'd never watch it": the power of
   investment).
3. **Flaws**: perfect people are unrelatable; Superman needs Kryptonite.
   Even typos humanize.
4. **Polarity**: take sides, draw lines, split the audience; neutral is
   invisible. Haters are the price of raving fans.
Four identities: Leader, Adventurer/Crusader, Reporter/Evangelist (start
here in a niche you don't know), Reluctant Hero (Brunson's default: shares
because of moral duty, not spotlight).
Six storylines: Loss and Redemption; Us vs Them (talkers vs do-ers);
Before and After; Amazing Discovery; Secret Telling (the lure of secrets
pulls people up the ladder); Third-Person Testimonial.

## Soap Opera Sequence (first 5 days on the list, auto-responder)

Open a loop in every email; close it in the next; open another. One or two
sentences per line, lots of white space.
1. **Set the Stage**: welcome, who I am, what to expect, open the loop
   ("tomorrow I'm giving you my best product free, BUT only if you open
   it"). P.S. names tomorrow's subject line verbatim.
2. **High Drama -> Backstory -> Wall**: start at the point of high drama
   (never the beginning), fill in the backstory that leads to the wall
   where they are stuck right now, promise the answer tomorrow.
3. **Epiphany**: the ah-ha that changed everything; the epiphany ties
   directly into the product being sold; CTA link.
4. **Hidden Benefits**: benefits they haven't thought of ("you'll lose
   weight, but you'll also live to see your grandchildren"); parables that
   demonstrate them.
5. **Urgency + CTA**: last push with REAL urgency (webinar tomorrow, seats
   left, price rises); fake urgency destroys credibility forever.

## Daily Seinfeld emails (day 6 forever, broadcast not sequence)

- 90% entertainment, 10% content. Brunson's switch from content-packed to
  Seinfeld style skyrocketed opens, clicks, and sales; daily beats weekly
  (each frequency increase doubled income).
- Emails about "nothing": what happened today, embarrassing moments, kid
  antics, rage-yesterday-laugh-today. BUT every story must tie back to an
  offer or the email makes nothing. Story -> pivot -> offer -> CTA.
- Both example emails in the book made $100k+ each: pure banter for half
  the email, then "why do I tell you this?" pivot into the pitch.
- Double duty: paste each Seinfeld email onto the blog as content.

## Reverse engineering competitors (before building ANY funnel)

Five variables: Demographics, Offer, Landing page, Traffic source, Ad copy.
NEVER move forward with two unknowns. Method: list direct AND indirect
competitors -> run their URLs through traffic tools -> collect their live
ads (long-running ad = winning ad) -> BUY their products and record every
upsell, downsell, and email. "Find what's already working and model it."
This is our competitor-intel doctrine stated 10 years earlier: the market
tells you the funnel; you don't invent it.

## Seven phases of a funnel (the diagnostic checklist)

Pre-frame rule: every step is a pre-frame for the next. The MIT study:
identical lecture, one word changed in the bio ("warm" vs "cold"), opposite
ratings. Control the frame your traffic walks through (Brunson's 3-minute
intro video before speaking = consistently higher stage sales).
1. **Traffic temperature**: hot (knows you), warm (knows someone you know:
   JV lift letters), cold (knows nothing). Gene Schwartz: start the copy
   where the prospect is: product aware -> lead with product; desire aware
   -> lead with desire; problem aware -> lead with problem.
2. **Pre-frame bridge**: hot = quick email; warm = endorsement note; cold =
   a full bridge page that renames jargon into their language (nerve pain,
   not neuropathy) and educates before the offer.
3. **Qualify subscribers**: squeeze page, one choice.
4. **Qualify buyers IMMEDIATELY**: Dan Kennedy: "a buyer is a buyer is a
   buyer." Cheap no-brainer offer right after opt-in, never a day later.
5. **Identify hyperactive buyers**: in-pain buyers buy several things RIGHT
   NOW (the bowling-loss story); offer upsells at once or they buy from
   someone else. The window closes in days.
6. **Age and ascend**: Value Ladder over time via email.
7. **Change the selling environment**: high ticket does not sell from a
   button; move to phone, direct mail, or events where a human can handle
   objections live.
Stuck business = a glitch in one of these seven. Diagnose in order.

## The 23 building blocks (assemble funnels Lego-style)

- Pre-frame bridge: quiz (also segments), article, news, blog post, video,
  email lift, presell page.
- Qualify subscribers: pop-up, squeeze page, squeeze pop, free-plus-
  shipping two-step form (email captured on step 1 even if they abandon
  step 2), webinar registration, free account, exit pop.
- Qualify buyers: free-plus-shipping, $1 trial, tripwire splinter ($7),
  self-liquidating offer ($37-97), straight sale.
- Identify hyperactives: order form bump (2 lines + checkbox after card
  info, before submit: 34-40% take rate), one-time offers (2-3), downsells
  (payment plan recovers ~20% of OTO nos), affiliate recommendations on the
  thank-you page.

## The best bait: the 100-visitor test

Straight sale $197 page: 1% conversion = $197 per 100 visitors, 1 buyer.
Free-plus-shipping splinter + same $197 as OTO: 8% take the free offer,
25% of those take the OTO = $394 per 100 visitors and EIGHT buyers.
Almost double the cash and 7x the buyer list. Free physical + card on file
= the word "free" + buyer qualification + one-click upsell. The free book
funnel averaged $66 immediate upsell revenue per free book given away.
First yes makes the second yes easy: the slippery slope of commitment.

## The seven core funnels and their scripts

1. **Two-Step Free-Plus-Shipping** (frontend, cold traffic). Page script =
   **Who What Why How**: who I am, what I've got, why you need it, how to
   get it, then The Catch (state why it's cheap: no catch), Urgency,
   Guarantee ("keep the DVD"), Recap. "Where should I ship this?"
2. **Self-Liquidating Offer** ($27-97; breaks even on ads BEFORE upsells).
   Page script = **Star Story Solution** (44 beats, the long-form sales
   letter/VSL): STAR: pattern interrupt, core-desire questions, agitate
   past failures, big promise/One Thing, introduce the star. STORY: high
   drama opening, backstory to the wall, identify the problem, epiphany or
   declaration, path to ultimate solution, first sign of success,
   conspiracy ("the cards were stacked against you"), the Big Lie ("it's
   not your fault"), common enemy, rapid growth, case studies, hidden
   benefits. SOLUTION: formal intro, pain and cost to create, ease, speed,
   "so" benefits (feature so benefit), social proof, make the offer, build
   value, float fake price, emotional close (If All it did was X... toward
   pleasure AND away from pain), reveal real price, named guarantee,
   inject scarcity, future pacing, CTA, post selling, take-away warning
   ("doesn't matter to us if you join"), close with recap for skimmers.
3. **Continuity** (second funnel in sequence, AFTER the Soap Opera bond:
   waiting dramatically raises stick rate). Trial via Who What Why How;
   no-trial via Star Story Solution; yearly/lifetime = best OTO.
4. **Perfect Webinar** (middle of ladder, 60-90 min, live once then
   automated forever; segment follow-up by show/stay/leave/no-show).
   INTRO: big promise (the ruler they judge by), hook to end (giveaway),
   command attention, qualify yourself, future pace. CONTENT: the One
   Thing framed "How to ___ without ___", then 3 secrets that BREAK and
   REBUILD the belief patterns blocking purchase. THE STACK: transition
   with "let me ask you a question"; re-stack the full offer list after
   every new component so the price anchors to EVERYTHING, not the last
   bonus (Armand Morin: took closes from 5-10% to 20%+); If/All anchor;
   reveal real price far below the stack total.
5. **Invisible Funnel** (premium webinar, pay AFTER if it was worth it).
   Registration = **Magic Bullet script**: raise desire questions, intro,
   point out frustrations (yours, which are theirs), the Magic Bullet
   ("get RESULT in TIME or pay nothing"), don't worry/proof, risk reversal
   (card now, judged later), close. Results: 85% show rate (vs ~30% free),
   90% happily billed, then a soft 3-step close (What/Why/How) into the
   high-ticket application. Content beats: What is it (cool name), Parable,
   Teach concept, Example; then recap, tie it to value, "was it worth $X?"
6. **Product Launch** (warm/hot lists, 4 videos over days):
   V1 Wow and How (big promise, kill myths, case studies, hook), V2
   Transformational Education (let's do it together, how I do it step by
   step, proof, hook), V3 Ownership Experience (future pace, how YOU would
   do it, hint at offer, hook), V4 The Offer (here's what I've got, what
   you'll do with it, what it'll do for you, what to do right now).
7. **High-Ticket Three-Step Application** (backend; replaced 60 phone reps
   with 2): free case-study video -> application page (the application
   makes THEM sell YOU on why they're a fit: pre-frames the yes) ->
   homework page (3 videos: class, personal why story, case studies; sets
   "we'll call you" and gives a call-us-now option for hyperactives).
   Phone = **Two-Step set/close script**, two salespeople, never the guru
   ("you're too close to the outcome; bad positioning"):
   SETTER: rapport, dig for the WHY under the goals (emotion first, logic
   later), "what's holding you back?" (every answer reduces to "I don't
   know how"), the pivotal question ("if you worked one-on-one with X, do
   you think you'd be successful? ...How come?": THEY state the reasons so
   it's true), posture ("I'm just here to find qualified people; the
   director decides"), probe financials + get ALL decision makers on,
   available credit math, goals at 6/12 months, four commitments (time,
   coachability, decide-today, OPM), hold for the "director".
   CLOSER: reconfirm dreams and commitments as clean yes/nos, investment
   number written down, teachability, what's included, and the close:
   "Why do you feel like you are a good candidate?": the prospect asks to
   buy instead of being asked.

## The OTO script (use for EVERY upsell everywhere)

Getting the second yes is 80% offer structure, 20% script. Structure rules:
never more of the same thing (the itch is scratched), never a random
product; use The Next Thing, Do It Faster, or Need Help. Script beats:
confirm the initial decision WITHOUT closing the loop ("Wait! Your order
is not quite finished": conversions rose when "order complete" was
removed), smart choice + why, 3x/2x question ("want 3x the results in half
the time?"), exclusive ("you proved you're an action taker"), THE ONE
THING (sell one strategy inside the product, never the full contents:
explaining everything kills the sale), future pacing, CTA, guarantee,
value stack, true scarcity ("gone when you leave this page"), second CTA,
testimonial rush. 3-5 minute videos close $97 to $2k+.

## Where this maps in our pipeline

- **Funnel copy deliverable**: pick the funnel by ladder position (free
  Skool = free bait; low-ticket = SLO; call funnel = High-Ticket 3-Step);
  our default client funnel IS the case study -> application -> homework
  structure. Application questions must make the prospect sell themselves.
- **VSL deliverable**: Star Story Solution beats cross-checked with the
  ACQ #14 flow and vsl-swipe-file.md; webinar VSLs use Perfect Webinar
  (One Thing + 3 belief breaks + Stack).
- **Email sequences**: Soap Opera Sequence IS our day 1-5 arc standard;
  Seinfeld doctrine (entertainment + daily + always tie to offer) merges
  with suby-email-machine.md for the ongoing broadcast plan we hand
  clients; segment by webinar behavior when there is a webinar.
- **Upsells/order pages**: OTO script + bump on every order form we spec;
  downsell = payment plan.
- **Foundation docs**: ICP section gets the congregation list (where they
  gather); offer doc maps the full Value Ladder, not just one offer;
  competitor research section follows the five-variables/never-two-
  unknowns method (pairs with our competitor intel tab).
- **Onboarding**: capture the client's Attractive Character (identity,
  backstory, flaws, polarity lines, story bank) so every email and VSL is
  written in that persona.
- **Skool posts/content**: Seinfeld style with one CTA; pre-frame bridge
  logic governs any cold-traffic advertorial or quiz page we recommend.
