# Hormozi $100M Closing Playbook (mined cover to cover)

How to turn nos into sales. Governs sales scripts, objection-handling docs,
FAQ sections, and the objection-crusher emails in every sequence. Pairs with
suby-doctor-close.md: Suby gives the call's spine, this gives the
objection-handling arsenal after the ask.

## The philosophy: power, not pressure

Advertising splits the audience into YES (laydowns: don't mess it up), NO
(unqualified: filter fast), and MAYBE (the fence: the money is in the
maybes). Maybes avoid deciding because they lack decision-making POWER.
Closing is not getting them to buy; it is giving them the power to make a
GENUINE decision either way. Letting someone stay on the avoidance treadmill
lets them down. Ethical floor (assume all three or do not close): the
product gets them to their goal, we support them, it works for THEM. The
difference between help and manipulation is intention.

## The Onion of Blame (every objection is one of three)

People dodge decisions by blaming, outside-in:
1. **Circumstances**: time ("I'm too busy"), money ("too expensive"),
   fit ("not right for me")
2. **Other people**: authority ("I need to check with X"),
   trust ("I've been burned before")
3. **Self**: fear ("it won't work for ME"), avoidance ("I need to think
   about it")
Prepare for people to say no MORE THAN ONCE; swapped objections mean the
close is working. Peel layers until the person can decide.

## Rules of closing (the ones that shape our scripts)

- When in doubt, repeat back what they said. Acknowledge or agree, NEVER
  disagree; every response opens with "totally understand" / "great point"
  then reframes.
- Before getting real, get permission: "Can I put my coach hat on for a
  second?" "Can I tell you what I told someone in your exact position?"
- Stack closes; know 2-3 per obstacle; understand, don't memorize.
- Yellows are golds: close every rep you ethically can; volume negates luck.
- Objections are often just out-loud processing, not nos. Price shock is
  not a no. "It's expensive" can be answered with "I agree."
- NEVER change price to get a sale. If they ask for less, "we could do it
  for more" (kills haggling instantly).
- Only ask when you think you've got them; selling happens before the ask,
  closing after; diffuse problems before asking for money.
- Once they say yes, SHUT UP.
- If offense is needed, tell it as a story about your past self or another
  client, never about them.
- Person who cares most about the prospect wins; kind beats nice.
- Qualify with STAR: Situation, Timing, Authority, Resources.
- Record and review every call like game tape.

## All-purpose closes (work on anything that's not yes)

- **Main Concern**: "What's your main concern? What are you afraid of
  happening?" Escalate to the absurd worst case to surface the real one.
- **Reason**: "The reason you're telling yourself not to do this is the
  reason you should." (No money -> exactly why you need this. No time ->
  same. Dependent on spouse -> reason to own a decision.)
- **Hypothetical**: "If this were perfect, would you do it? What's the
  difference between perfect and what we've got?" Fix the small detail or
  expose the real fear.
- **Zoom Out**: "You want X. We sell X. Is overanalyzing the details maybe
  why you haven't made it happen yet?"
- **1 to 10**: "Where are you, 1-10?" 9 or less: "what would it take to get
  you to a 10?" Fix that. "Feeling 10ish now?"
- **Best Case Worst Case**: with a guarantee, every option is risk-free
  except walking out the door, which GUARANTEES they don't get what they
  want. "Which risk-free option do you want?"
- **Card Not On Me**: ID-first mechanic (wallet comes out naturally); ACH
  fallback: "pull up your banking app, account number top right."

## Circumstance closes

Time: Better To Start When You're Busy (if it only works in easy seasons it
won't last), You're Gonna Get Busy Again, It's Not Timing It's Priorities
("either the goal outranks the interruptions or it doesn't; if it doesn't,
I'd rather you leave knowing"), Smartphone close (20 hrs/week of social
media found = "I guess I found you some time"), When/Then flip ("when I
have time" = "when I'm healthy, then I'll see the doctor").

Money, four flavors:
1. Not enough value: translate "price too high" into "VALUE too low" and
   rebuild value. It's Good That It's A Lot (the stake makes you take it
   seriously; the real risk is staying where you are), Good Things Aren't
   Cheap, Would You Even Believe Me If It Were Less?, If All It Did Was X,
   Not What You Make But What It's Worth, You're Gonna Spend The Money
   Either Way, You Pay The Price Either Way (time or money; buying time
   back at a discount), Some Now Or More Later, Future Favor (make the next
   10 years the decision-changed ones).
2. Can't afford it: Resourcefulness Not Resources (self-made millionaires
   started with nothing but resourcefulness; Phil Knight had vendors pay
   his payroll), Everyone Starts At Zero, life-saving-surgery nudge.
3. Guy down the street does it for less: Cheap Comparison ("if we were the
   same price which would you pick? why? ...so now you see why we're not
   the same price"), Good Fast Cheap pick two ("most expensive option is
   the fast-and-cheap guy, then hiring me anyway"), Cheap Or What You Need.
4. Haggling: "We could do it for more." As soon as they'd take it for less,
   ALL non-price objections are surrendered ("It's Not, Not The Money").

## Other-people closes (partner/permission)

Best avoided: qualify decision makers onto the call; reschedule otherwise.
Ladder: "What if they say no?" (1 in 3 say "I'd do it anyway": close) ->
"What specifically would they object to? The price, the program, or me?"
(off-ramp to that obstacle) -> "Does your spouse approve of your current
struggle? Then why would they block you fixing it?" -> role-reversal nudge
("if he needed this for HIS dream, would you support him?") -> support not
permission ("give power away and in two years, who gets blamed? That's
resentment; not fair to you or them") -> sign now + delayed payment + tear-
up-the-contract promise (three-day no-sweat). Burned before: "Don't let a
bad decision burn you twice: once when you made it, twice if it blocks the
good one. Bad eighth-grade boyfriend doesn't mean all men are out."

## Self-blame closes

Preferences ("I want it MY way"): Isolate And Solve FIRST ("what
specifically makes it not for you?"), We Vote With Our Dollars (Sephora
story: new identity = new priorities), Pain Of Change vs Pain Of Same,
You Gotta Change To Change ("that breakfast makes you look like you"),
Change The Variables Change The Outcome, Mechanic close ("diagnosing before
we look under the hood would be malpractice; anyone who answers that before
knowing you: run"), Surgeon Secretary alternate.

Rushed decisions ("let me think about it"): What Would Make It A No? (weak
nos expose that they're simply unsure, like every great customer before
them), Don't Repeat The Mistake That Got You Here, How Much Has NOT
Deciding Cost You? (we regret the things we didn't do), Another Year Of
Almost (almost 10k/mo, almost retired her, almost the neighborhood... "what
about a THIS IS MY YEAR kinda year?"), It Doesn't Take Time To Make
Decisions It Takes Information ("you won't sit in a rocking chair
deliberating; life happens, jeans don't fit, decision made; I'm the only
source of information you've got, so let's talk"), Gameplan close (3
questions: closer to goals? access to money? better with us than alone?),
This Isn't A Fast Decision ("you made it years ago; you're six inches from
gold"). Guarantee-backed: Both Options Are Risk Free ("I'm only asking you
to make an INFORMED decision, which you can only make after trying it"),
Decadere (decide = de-cadere, to cut off: "which future are you cutting
off?"), You're Not Gonna Struggle Forever Right?, Closer Or Further
("directionally right beats sniper").

## Where this maps in our pipeline

- **Sales scripts deliverable**: objection-handling section is built from
  the Onion of Blame: 2-3 closes per obstacle, phrased in the client's
  voice with the client's proof.
- **Funnel FAQ sections**: each FAQ answer is a written close (Reason,
  Best Case Worst Case, Burned Before, Cost of Inaction).
- **Email objection-crusher days**: pick the market's top 2 objections
  from research and write the matching close as a story email.
- **VSL close section**: Decadere / Closer-or-Further / Another Year of
  Almost are ethical one-to-many closers before the CTA.
- **Guarantee copy**: guarantee closes only work if the offer HAS a
  guarantee: hormozi-offers.md guarantee build feeds this.
