# Hormozi Bonus Chapters + Lost Chapter (mined cover to cover)

Two sources: the $100M bonus chapters (Premium / Free / Discount promotion
wrappers) and the Lost Chapter (Your First Avatar). Governs how we frame
front-end offers, lead magnets, low-ticket entries, and how the ICP section
of every foundation doc gets sharpened.

## Promotion wrappers (Attract)

A promotion ENHANCES the Grand Slam Offer, it never changes it. Same gift,
better wrapping paper. Its job: give people who otherwise wouldn't respond
a reason to respond. Operating order: **Get flow -> Monetize flow -> THEN
add friction.** Generate demand first; figure out monetization second; add
qualification hoops last.

### Premium (offer stands alone at full price)

The $50,000 burger: price against the buyer's economics, not yours. Math:
$10,000 offer at 5% close beats a $100 offer at scale by ~9.5x profit with
1/20th the clients. Requirements: a true Grand Slam Offer, a dialed sales
process, GREAT copy (premium leaves no margin for lazy copy: specificity is
the edge, "cleaning the bathrooms yet again" not "working hard"), and an
offer so stacked it still feels like a deal. Fewer, better clients; least
efficient for volume. If starting from zero, do NOT lead premium.

### Free (the most powerful offer of all time)

The penny gap: free pulls ~9x more takers than one cent. Free = highest
volume + lowest cost per lead; split tests showed close rates and ticket
size were IDENTICAL for free vs non-free respondents, but free cut lead
cost ~5x. "Free brings broke people" is a myth in the data. If a free offer
flops, the diagnosis is: they don't want it (change it), they don't believe
you (answer WHY it's free: the skepticism box), or wrong pond (targeting).
Manage volume with FRICTION, tuned not maximized:
1. Qualification requirements stated in the copy (repeat them everywhere)
2. More/heavier application questions (phone + income = heavy; multiple
   choice = lighter than open text)
3. More steps (each step drops volume, raises quality)
4. Forced consumption (must watch the VSL before the CTA appears)
5. Ad/asset length (longer pre-CTA consumption = better leads)
Hormozi: "If I had ONE offer to convert or die, it would be a free offer."

### Discount (massive only)

Marginal discounts (5-25%) don't move behavior; go 50%+ or don't bother.
Discount a SPLINTER of the offer, never the core (discounting the core
trains people to wait for sales). Killer feature: the TWO-STEP SALE: cheap
paid entry ($19-$97) puts a card on file, kills no-shows (85-90%+ show
when they paid), and makes the real sale an upsell to an existing customer
instead of a first sale to a stranger. More steps = more time with us =
more trust = higher prices feel pre-imagined, not split-second. Discounts
work best on well-understood services where the full price is known.

## Your First Avatar (the Vista method)

Vista's billion-dollar play: score current customers by who pays the most
and stays the longest, cut the channels that bring low-value customers,
double down on the channels that bring the best. 80/20 on steroids.

The four steps (this is our onboarding/ICP sharpening method):
1. **Survey customers**: demographics, business stats, aspirations, and
   BUYING PROCESS (the single biggest reason they bought, the trigger
   event, which content they consumed, first-touch vs purchase timing,
   referral source).
2. **Find the biggest spenders**: sort by like-most, spent-most,
   stayed-longest. Top 20% only; ignore the rest.
3. **Find what they share**: the FEWEST qualifiers they all have in common
   (usually 3-5).
4. **Execute**: (a) speak ONLY to that avatar and state the requirements
   in the ads/pages (repel bad, attract good, stop selling non-fits);
   (b) reverse-engineer the best customers' buying journey and force it on
   purpose (Gym Launch: 78% of top customers consumed 2+ long-form content
   pieces before buying, so two value pieces were injected into every
   buyer journey and the sales team armed with "greatest hits" content).

Key laws:
- You make more because of who THEY are, not who you are: same work on a
  bigger client is worth 10-100x (the sales-page 10%-of-growth example).
- Quality > quantity: removing qualification steps raises volume and
  LOWERS profit. "I'd rather pay $5,000 to acquire $45,000 than $1,000 to
  acquire $5,000."
- Sell to people who don't stop buying.
- No customers yet? Start with the industry you know best, narrowest
  target you can help the most, re-run the analysis once data exists.

## Where this maps in our pipeline

- **Foundation ICP doc**: add best-customer qualifiers (the 3-5 shared
  traits) and the reverse-engineered buying journey when client data
  exists; onboarding questions should capture the Buying Process set.
- **Funnel front-end**: free named session is our default Godfather offer
  (free wrapper); friction tuned via the qualification survey.
- **Skool free community**: the free offer; pinned content = forced
  consumption; entry questions = friction dial.
- **Low-ticket / trial offers in emails**: two-step sale mechanics; card
  on file + show-rate logic; discount a splinter, never the core.
- **Ad copy**: state avatar requirements verbatim in the copy to repel
  non-buyers (pairs with suby-sell-the-click.md).
